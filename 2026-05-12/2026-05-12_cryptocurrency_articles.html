The enduring appeal of gold and oil is not simply a rational response to market performance. People consistently favor assets they understand, can visualize, and relate to culturally. Economists call this “familiarity bias”— the tendency to prefer investments that feel psychologically close and socially recognizable. It represented dowries, portable wealth, family insurance, and intergenerational savings. “Gold has traditionally been viewed as a store of value and a hedge against inflation,” says George Naddaf, Managing Director at eToro MENA. “That's how UAE investors see gold in general, while oil is closely tied to the regional economic narrative.” It can be held, stored, worn, inherited, and trusted. Source: {'href': 'https://www.tradingview.com', 'title': 'TradingView'}    Published: 2026-05-12 07:06:20

The Ethereum Foundation has reached several progress milestones on the next Ethereum upgrade called “Glamsterdam” and has named three new leads for its Protocol team. Glamsterdam focuses on scaling the layer-1 chain by reorganizing how the network processes transactions and manages its growing database, “fundamentally updating how Ethereum creates and verifies blocks,” according to the Ethereum website. “Glamsterdam devnets are now live, and scoping for Hegotà is well underway,” it stated during an interop event in Svalbard, Norway. The EF also confirmed the stabilization of enshrined Proposer-Builder Separation (ePBS), a system that allows validators to outsource their block-building duties to a set of specialized builders. The new enshrined version builds this separation directly into Ethereum's rules with less reliance on outside relays, giving the network more time to handle bigger blocks safely. EIP-8037 has also been finalized, which enables smarter pricing for storing data. The proposal increases the cost of state creation operations, avoiding excessive state growth under increased block gas limits. Glamsterdam is the first upgrade on Ethereum's long-term roadmap. Ethereum developers Barnabé Monnot and Tim Beiko are moving on from the Foundation, while Alex Stokes will be on sabbatical, it said. “There's a new chapter starting for the Protocol cluster. “Making Ethereum's unique features more available to users today is on my mind; so is participating in the plurality of ways that Ethereum gets built,” said Monnot.
