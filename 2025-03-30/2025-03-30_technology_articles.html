What was never told about bread for breakfast: insights from endocrinology
Source: gizmodo    Published: 2025-03-30 18:08:05

Bread is a common food in Spanish nutrition, with over 92% of the population consuming it regularly. According to the Pan Cada Día Association, 75% choose white bread, while others opt for toast with tomatoes or fresh sandwiches. Endocrinologist Francisco Rosero warns that breaking the fasting period with bread can have negative effects on glucose levels and offer little nutritional value. During nighttime fasting, the body enters an optimal metabolic state to use fat reserves as energy sources, but consuming a fast carbohydrate source like bread stops this process abruptly. The increase in insulin production favors fat storage, makes weight loss difficult, and causes problems such as obesity or type 2 diabetes. Consuming bread on an empty stomach can be particularly harmful to people with existing metabolic disorders. For those with insulin resistance or increased glucose mirrors, the body has difficulty changing sugar, and consuming bread forces the pancreas to produce more insulin. This overload can cause a chain reaction of metabolic problems. The fatty liver is also affected by excess carbohydrates in bread, which are converted into triglycerides and stored in adipose tissue, worsening the disease. Bread, especially white bread, is poor in fiber and micronutrients. Although sourdough bread is considered healthier, it contains almost the same carbohydrates and calories as white bread. Its glycemic index is only slightly lower (60 compared to 70), and it delivers a little more fiber but not enough to alleviate its negative effects when consumed at the wrong time. The gluten content remains in sourdough bread, which can be problematic for people with sensitivity or intolerance. There is no solid evidence that sourdough bread is significantly healthier than white bread. Foods such as eggs, avocados, nuts, or unsweetened natural yogurt offer proteins, healthy fats, and fiber, keeping glucose levels stable and ensuring more satisfaction. The ideal breakfast does not depend on taste or habits but on how the body reacts after hours of fasting. Bread is not the best ally to start the day well.
