The Biggest Mysteries In The Zelda Universe That Still Haven't Been Solved
Source: {'href': 'https://www.thegamer.com', 'title': 'TheGamer'}    Published: 2025-09-15 19:15:00

The Legend of Zelda series has never been known for coherent lore. Heck, one look at the timeline (if you can even call it one) and you'll see that the developers don't exactly have lore and narrative at the forefront of their minds when making these games. Having said that, it's still fun to dive into the story of the Zelda games and piece together what we do have. In doing that, the fan community has come across a lot of mysteries that are yet to be solved in any game. These are the biggest of those mysteries, and my best guess at what the answers to them might be. Its most obvious mystery, however, is the titular Majora's Mask itself. Beyond the mask itself, you have to wonder how the Happy Mask Salesman got his hands on it. I'd put my money on the fact that he had some hand in it all, personally. He doesn't seem innocent to me. In classic Zelda fashion, Tears of the Kingdom didn't answer all our questions from Breath of the Wild. For the Hyrule Royal Family line to continue after Rauru and Sonia are killed, they must have had a child offscreen. Why are they never with their parents? Maybe Hyrule Warriors: Age of Imprisonment will tell us, but I don't have high hopes. Once Link returns to his childhood, Navi flies off. Link goes to look for her, which leads him into the Lost Woods and the events of Majora's Mask. Is she off on another adventure now, with some other child who needs her? One of the big twists revealed in Skyward Sword is that Ghirahim is to Demise what Fi is to Link: a companion residing in a powerful sword. The Master Sword is an important part of Zelda lore and features in almost every game since its debut, so where is Demise's sword? Ganondorf doesn't really have a signature weapon like Link does, so we could assume it's the same sword, but this has never been confirmed. Twilight Princess is confirmed to be a sequel to Ocarina of Time, with Ganondorf in both games being the very same man, and Twilight Princess Link being a descendant of the Hero of Time. However, despite this connection, one aspect of Hyrule is very different: the Hyrule in Twilight Princess has no Gerudo. Instead of Gerudo Valley, in its place is Arbiter's Grounds. Twilight Princess doesn't tell us anything, so we'll just have to make up our own interpretation of events. At the end of Link's Awakening, it's revealed that Koholint Island, and everyone on it, was part of the Wind Fish's dream. Marin also ‚Äòbecomes' real by turning into a seagull, which was her wish. Was she always real (just not in the form of a girl), or was her wish so strong that she became real anyway? We never see Marin again, so we'll never find out if she was a dream-turned-real, or if she was real all along. I'd like to think she was a dream who became real, personally. It's a lot more whimsical that way. Well, we have another whopper from Majora's Mask. If you collect every single mask in the game, you can trade them in and get the Fierce Deity Mask, which transforms Link into an adult and gives him the Fierce Deity Sword. Aside from it being extremely powerful, we have next to no information about what the Fierce Deity actually is. Is it from the same place as Majora's Mask? Does the Fierce Deity actually look like that, or is it just adapting to Link's form? At every stable, and in lots of towns, you can find adorable dogs. But, I hear you asking, what about cats? Apparently, Calamity Ganon wiped out every single cat that ever lived in Hyrule. There's not a single one in the entire game, and they don't come back in Tears of the Kingdom either. Seriously, why is this world completely devoid of cats? Breath of the Wild has no such joys. I don't care about where the dragons came from or where BotW is on the timeline - I want to know where all the cats have gone! Frank said there are way too many unknowns to make any sort of scientific declaration based on what he described as a "grainy video with blobs on it that comes with a predigested story." "If you really want to know what it is, you've got to do a proper investigation of it," he said. "That means knowing what time of day is it? The other thing to take into account, Frank said, was that the object was not moving very fast since Hellfire Missiles are used mainly to attack stationary or slower-moving objects like tanks and helicopters. But the biggest takeaway from my discussion with Frank was something I hadn't noticed on the video or even considered. "It's not like you see the missile hit whatever the thing was and explode in a giant explosion and then the thing propagates away from it like in one of those science fiction movies," Frank said. "It looks like it's a glancing blow right which means that the targeting wasn't that great. You see it move, so this is not some kind of super alien technology which is impervious to anything, it kind of behaves like you might expect something to behave if it got a glancing blow from a fast moving object." OK, so maybe it's not from outer space. Florida Congresswoman and chair of Declassification of Federal Secrets Anna Paulina Luna, R-Florida, wants to know as well. She has been calling on the government to be more transparent when it comes to these types of things. "From Roswell to the coast of Jacksonville, the sightings of UFO's have rarely been explained by the people who have first-hand accounts of these situations," she said last year. "This is largely due to the lack of transparency by our own government and the failure of our elected leaders to make good on their promises to release explanations and footage and mountains of classified documents that continue to be hidden from the American people." I've only recently come across the term ‚ÄúPeer State Adversary,‚Äù meaning a country with similar technology as ours that is adversarial. Anyway, more likely than an alien spacecraft, the object on the video could be a drone operated by one of those adversarial countries. And if that's the case, Frank explains, the military would have good reason to keep tight-lipped about it. For example, Frank said, what if a country like China or Iran was testing the camera-spoofing technology of an experimental drone? Our military leaders would not want to tip their hand and reveal to those nations what we know about them. ‚ÄúAs somebody who studies the possibility of life in the universe professionally, I would love if we found aliens," Frank said. "That would answer the question I've been interested in my whole life. But there's just nothing there to be able to make that giant leap without the right kind of scientific evidence."
