Donald Trump announced Friday that the draw for the 2026 men's World Cup will be held on 5 December at Washington's Kennedy Center – and suggested he may even take part himself. “It's the biggest, probably the biggest event in sports,” said Trump, 79, wearing a red hat reading: Trump Was Right About Everything. Infantino likened the month-long tournament to “104 Super Bowls”, prompting Trump to add: “Some of them are bigger than Super Bowls, actually.” The tournament begins on 11 June at Mexico City's Azteca Stadium and ends on 19 July at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey. Washington is not among the 16 host cities, but Trump has turned the Kennedy Center into his personal fiefdom, installing himself as chair, replacing its board with loyalists and floating the name “Trump-Kennedy Center”. The president fumbled momentarily before placing it on his desk. “Can I keep it?” Infantino later presented him with a giant ticket marked Row 1, Seat 1 for the final. Asked if he would draw the team names himself, Trump smiled and deferred to Infantino. “It's a very interesting proposal,” the Fifa chief replied. Trump went further, suggesting Russian president Vladimir Putin “wants to be there very badly” for the finals, though “it may depend on the outcome of peace efforts in Ukraine.” The Kennedy Center, now undergoing a $257m renovation, will double as Fifa's new US headquarters. Trump has called it a showpiece for America's 250th anniversary celebrations next year. Fifa has also expanded its US presence, opening offices in Miami and New York's Trump Tower.
