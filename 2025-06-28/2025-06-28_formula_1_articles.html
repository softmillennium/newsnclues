F2: Josep Maria Martí wins for Campos Racing in dramatic Sprint Race
Source: {'href': 'https://www.formula1.com', 'title': 'F1'}    Published: 2025-06-28 16:39:15

‘It's been a very smooth weekend' – Norris revels in ‘the best feeling' after taking pole Josep María Martí took his second race victory of the 2025 Formula 2 season in a dramatic Austrian Sprint Race. Josep María Martí took his second race victory of the 2025 Formula 2 season after a Sprint Race full of drama at the Red Bull Ring. The Campos Racing driver started from fifth after receiving a grid penalty following Qualifying on Friday, but was up to second with a superb launch and opening few corners. After a prolonged battle with front-row starter Joshua Duerksen, Martí made an overtake stick on the AIX Racing driver to take the lead and he went on to finish P1, almost three seconds clear of the pack. The race was red-flagged in the early laps following a multi-car collision at Turn 3. Sami Meguetounif was launched into the air after contact with Arvid Lindblad, and he collected Luke Browning in the process. Into the closing stages, Martí made his move at Turns 3 and 4, sealed the lead and never looked back. Having started second, Duerksen finished on the podium in P2, holding off Invicta Racing's Roman Stanek despite clipping the gravel just a few corners from the chequered flag. Elsewhere, Alexander Dunne's advantage in the Drivers' Championship was reduced to one point over Richard Verschoor after the MP Motorsport driver finished fourth, with Dunne finishing sixth.

Dunne 'grateful' for 'pretty special' McLaren outing in Austria after impressive fourth in FP1
Source: {'href': 'https://www.formula1.com', 'title': 'F1'}    Published: 2025-06-28 09:29:27

‘It's been a very smooth weekend' – Norris revels in ‘the best feeling' after taking pole Alex Dunne hailed his Formula 1 practice debut with McLaren, for the Austrian Grand Prix, as "something pretty special." Alex Dunne has hailed his Formula 1 practice debut as "something pretty special" after finishing only fractionally slower than the sister McLaren of Oscar Piastri during FP1 ahead of the Austrian Grand Prix. Although Dunne previously sampled McLaren's 2023 challenger and underwent simulator running, this was his first time in an official F1 session. Despite his relative lack of experience, Dunne posted the fourth fastest time at the Red Bull Ring in FP1, less than a tenth behind championship leader Piastri in the sister McLaren, and just over two tenths down on George Russell's session-best. "I don't even know where to start," said Dunne after the session. McLaren Team Principal Andrea Stella was impressed by Dunne's performance, not only because of his speed but also that he was able to help the team develop the car, which featured a new suspension set-up. This in turn helped regular driver Lando Norris once he got back behind the wheel of his car, before topping FP2 ahead of Piastri. "I think we have to acknowledge that he [Dunne] has gone through quite an extensive plan of testing with some new parts, with some rake work, some adjustments on the steering wheel that we don't see on TV but definitely keeps the drivers very busy," Stella told Sky Sports F1. "I think we need to be a bit careful looking at the lap times because his time came later on in the stint when the fuel was down but I think encouraging and impressive in terms of Alex himself and also I think a good session for McLaren."
