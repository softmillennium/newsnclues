ABM history is the beginning of precision targeting Account-based marketing (ABM) emerged in the 1990s as a strategy for business (B2B) marketing with corporate customers. Unlike traditional mass marketing, ABM was initially established as an approach to developing individual marketing initiatives, focusing on specific companies (accounts), and attracted attention as a method that allows for high return on investment (ROI) by sending customized messages to target accounts. This marketing method is known as "precision targeting" (hyperprecision targeting), and has begun the method of delivering the most suitable advertising and promotions to the target audience. Precision targeting improves advertising click-through rates and conversion rates, contributes to customer re-engagement, increases engagement and lifetime value, and enables marketing to be more efficient. As the 2000s began, ABM became even more refined with the evolution of customer relationship management (CRM) systems and marketing automation (MA) tools. Companies will be able to integrate customer data and adopt a more personalized approach. However, in reality, despite its ideal, a packaged CRM requires the support of a large consultant, or it is not easy to meet the needs of individual companies due to packaging constraints, and sometimes the development of scratch costs can be incurred, resulting in many manual processes, making it extremely difficult to deploy large scale. The transition and challenges to data-driven marketing Currently, ABM is becoming more advanced with advances in digital technology. With the use of AI and big data analytics, businesses can understand intent data that indicates potential customers' purchasing intentions and take a more precise approach. On the other hand, there are several challenges when looking ahead to ABM.
