Why Liverpool is not playing in Club World Cup despite winning Premier League title
Source: {'href': 'https://www.liverpool.com', 'title': 'Liverpool.com'}    Published: 2025-04-30 05:15:00

Liverpool may be the new champion of England, but that won't be enough to give it a shot at being crowned the world champion this summer. Having secured a 20th league title, the Reds are now back on their perch, as Sir Alex Ferguson would say, after going back level with Manchester United's tally. And, no matter what Rio Ferdinand says, there can be no debate now about who the most successful team in England is, with Liverpool's next task being to go beyond its Old Trafford rival once again. Despite boasting more silverware than any other team in England, Liverpool won't be traveling to the United States to represent the Premier League in this summer's revamped Club World Cup. FIFA's expanded tournament gets its first run-out in June, with 32 teams from the six continental confederations set to battle it out to be crowned champions of the world. As you may have noticed though, that criteria doesn't cover every club. In fact, it only applies to three of the 16 teams from UEFA. Additional places are determined by a club ranking over the four-year period, with FIFA imposing a two-team-per-nation rule, hence why Liverpool misses out despite reaching the 2022 Champions League final. In truth though, Liverpool shouldn't be too disappointed with its snub by FIFA. One of his current squad could be involved in the US though, with Real Madrid reportedly hoping to have Trent Alexander-Arnold available for the tournament - assuming he doesn't choose to extend his stay at Anfield.
