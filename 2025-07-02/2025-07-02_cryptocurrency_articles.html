Will Bitcoin benefit from ‘Big Beautiful Bill' passage and US debt ceiling increase?
Source: {'href': 'https://cointelegraph.com', 'title': 'Cointelegraph'}    Published: 2025-07-02 19:40:00

Historical data fail to show a consistent link between Bitcoin price gains and US debt ceiling increases. United States Senators successfully advanced President Trump's ‘One Big Beautiful Bill' on Tuesday, moving it one step closer to becoming law. Although several solid analyses point to a bullish outlook for Bitcoin, past US debt ceiling increases and suspensions have generally led to bearish outcomes, at least in the six months that followed. However, that assumption weakens when looking at Bitcoin's flat performance. On Tuesday, Bitcoin held steady at $105,000, the same level as five months earlier. At that time, economists projected the government would run out of funds by mid-August. Sven Henrich, founder of NorthmanTrader, criticized US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent's claims that the bill represents a step toward “controlling the US debt.” According to Henrich, raising the debt ceiling while “running record deficits” and lowering interest rates aligns with “modern monetary theory”—an approach suggesting that governments can fund expenditures by creating money, rather than through taxes or borrowing. Rather than focusing solely on lawmakers' decisions, attention should turn to how the central bank will respond. If the US Federal Reserve maintains higher interest rates, debt servicing costs rise. Generally speaking, higher US Treasury yields reflect reduced investor confidence, as buyers demand greater compensation for perceived risks. Historically, this indicator has shown a positive correlation with Bitcoin's price, meaning both tend to rise together, given the cryptocurrency's appeal as an alternative asset. Therefore, Bitcoin holding above $105,000 while the 10-year Treasury yield fell to 4.25% from 4.50% on June 6 suggests early signs of a decoupling. Even so, it remains too early to declare Bitcoin a proven reserve asset, particularly as both gold and the S&P 500 approach their own all-time highs. Ultimately, while the debt ceiling increase may coincide with a Bitcoin rally above $110,000, historical patterns do not support a direct causal link between these events. BlackRock's iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT) is now generating more revenue than one of the asset manager's most iconic products, the iShares Core S&P 500 ETF (IVV), according to Bloomberg data. The rapid growth of IBIT highlights the ongoing demand for regulated bitcoin investment products, particularly those offered by established financial firms like BlackRock. While IBIT's management fee is higher than more traditional ETFs, it reflects the added complexity, custody and regulatory requirements involved in offering exposure to a digital asset like bitcoin. By tokenizing carbon credits, the partnership aims to unlock trillions in climate finance while addressing systemic inefficiencies that have stifled institutional participation. For investors, this represents a rare convergence of innovation, regulatory momentum, and existential demand. Voluntary carbon credits—financial instruments representing avoided or removed CO₂ emissions—are central to corporate net-zero pledges and climate accountability. Liquidity Constraints: Illiquid markets deter institutional investors seeking scalable, tradable assets. These issues create a “greenwashing risk premium” that deters capital, even as demand for credible climate solutions surges. S&P Global and Kinexys' exploratory pilot, set to launch in July 2025, targets these pain points head-on. The collaboration leverages S&P Global's Environmental Registry—a trusted platform for carbon project data—and integrates it with Kinexys' blockchain infrastructure. A system that turns carbon credits into programmable, tokenized assets, governed by smart contracts and recorded on an immutable ledger. Key Innovations:- Standardization: A unified data model ensures credits from reforestation, renewable energy, or carbon capture projects are comparable.- Fraud Prevention: Blockchain's “single source of truth” eliminates double-counting and ensures credits are retired upon purchase.- Streamlined Settlements: Cross-chain capabilities enable frictionless transfers, mirroring the efficiency of traditional financial markets. Tokenization creates an audit trail, enabling investors to verify a credit's origin, additionality, and permanence. This aligns with growing regulatory scrutiny—e.g., the EU's proposed Carbon Removal Certification Framework—and rising ESG accountability demands. By digitizing credits, the partnership taps into institutional demand for “real-world asset” (RWA) tokenization. Tokenized credits directly support Article 6 of the Paris Agreement, which mandates integrity and transparency in carbon markets. Institutional capital flowing into these instruments could accelerate the voluntary market's growth to over $2 trillion by 2030—a trajectory amplified by corporate net-zero commitments and sovereign climate bonds. Regulatory divergence between regions like the EU and U.S. could fragment standards, while overhyping tokenization may lead to speculative bubbles.
