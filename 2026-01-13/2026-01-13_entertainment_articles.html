The Dilbert principle — traced back to a quote in a 1995 strip — posited that managers and higher-ups are actually successful morons whose stubbornness is confused for real leadership qualities. “[I]n many cases the least competent, least smart people are promoted, simply because they're the ones you don't want doing actual work,” Adams said in a 2002 interview.
