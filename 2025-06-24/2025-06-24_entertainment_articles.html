The claims in Dixon's Thursday (June 19) complaint, which Fat Joe has fiercely denied and characterized as extortion, are framed as a racketeering enterprise abetted by Roc Nation and other outside entities. “Yet, according to plaintiff, the fact that Roc Nation had a business relationship with Mr. Cartagena somehow makes Roc Nation part of a RICO [Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act] enterprise, a participant in sex trafficking, and an aider-and-abettor of Mr. Cartagena's alleged conduct. Dixon's lawsuit claimed the management company was responsible for his withheld wages, but Spiro says Roc Nation only manages Fat Joe's catalog, publishing rights and intellectual property. “How Roc Nation supposedly knew of this deplorable alleged conduct is a mystery,” writes Spiro. “He does not assert how Roc Nation's management of Mr. Cartagena's music business gave it insight into how much plaintiff should have been paid, what he was paid, or any venture to deprive him of adequate compensation.” Reached for comment on Monday, Dixon's attorney Tyrone Blackburn tells Billboard he will oppose Roc Nation's motion to be dismissed from the case. “The plaintiff has no personal animosity towards Roc Nation or its leadership.” Two months ago, Fat Joe filed a lawsuit accusing the former hypeman and his attorney of trying to extract a settlement with “wholly fabricated” and “grotesque” claims of pedophilia.
