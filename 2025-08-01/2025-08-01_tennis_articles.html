A new dating show, “Game, Set, Matchmaker,” is “set to unfold entirely within the electrifying atmosphere” of the U.S. Open in N.Y., according to Stephanie Livaudais of TENNIS. Part reality TV, part social experiment, the show follows one bachelorette as she “seeks real connection amid Grand Slam chaos.” Produced by the U.S. Open and “set to premiere” on YouTube, the “unscripted series is breaking ground” at the intersection of sports, pop culture and romance -- and “aims to tap into the cultural phenomenon launched by shows like ‘Love Island USA,' ‘Love Is Blind' and ‘The Bachelor' in recent years.” At the “center of it all” is “the Champion” -- 24-year-old former figure skater turned Pilates coach Ilana Sedaka. Seven contestants “ranging from influencers and social media personalities to tennis superfans will compete for Sedaka's affection in a fast-paced” series. The dating show is “a collaboration” between the USTA, production company Fresh Tape Media, and Alissa Danzig and Brian Pollack of CastingHaus. The USTA sees the show as a “fresh way to expand the tournament's cultural reach.” The series will be filmed during U.S. Open Fan Week (Aug. 18-23) at the Billie Jean King National Tennis Center. However, Italian outlet Corriere della Sera has now reported that Sinner had grown increasingly uncomfortable with certain decisions made by Panichi, both on and off the court. One of the trigger points was reportedly Panichi's decision to leak confidential team information to the media. Panichi and Badio, both of whom previously worked with Novak Djokovic, came under scrutiny after their dismissal. Panichi, in particular, was seen as a divisive figure in Sinner's camp, with reports suggesting he frequently defied team rules that restricted public statements without prior clearance. Sinner, a four-time Grand Slam champion, initially downplayed speculation about the departures. We reached incredible results in the past with them. We spend a lot of time together throughout the year, and it's important to find the right people,” he explained. The 23-year-old has since brought back Umberto Ferrara, who had left the team last year following an investigation into Sinner's two failed doping tests.
