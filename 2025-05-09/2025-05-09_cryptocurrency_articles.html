Crypto sleuth ZachXBT says wrong suspect detained in Bored Ape NFT theft
Source: {'href': 'https://cointelegraph.com', 'title': 'Cointelegraph'}    Published: 2025-05-09 19:14:22

Law enforcement detained the wrong person for a 2022 scam that pilfered more than $1 million worth of Bored Ape non-fungible tokens (NFTs), cybersecurity researcher ZachXBT said. In a May 9 X post, ZachXBT said he identified the wallet behind the scam and linked it to an X account that has since been deleted. But in 2023, law enforcement detained Sam Curry, a former Yuga Labs security researcher, at an airport as a suspect in the incident, ZachXBT said. In December 2022, an anonymous attacker stole 14 Bored Ape Yacht Club NFTs, which at the time traded for roughly $86,000 each, according to data from CoinSlam. Law enforcement then “mistakenly reviewed logs from OpenSea which included [Curry's] home IP address and used this to incorrectly link him as the suspect,” ZachXBT wrote. ZachXBT said he used forensic tracing — including reconstructing the flow of funds through Tornado, an Ethereum mixer — to identify a person he alleges is a likely suspect in the 2022 theft. He said law enforcement officials should “request all data related to [the individual's] social media accounts” and dig into on-chain transactions associated with their alleged wallet. Launched in 2021, Bored Ape Yacht Club is among the most valuable NFT collections, with a cumulative market capitalization of more than $300 million, according to data from CoinGecko. As of May 9, Bored Ape NFTs trade for roughly $30,000 each, according to NFT marketplace OpenSea. Blockchain for sustainable packaging: a game changer for the environment
Source: {'href': 'https://www.yahoo.com', 'title': 'Yahoo'}    Published: 2025-05-09 08:43:34

In recent years, the global demand for sustainable practices has grown, with industries across the world rethinking how they can reduce their environmental impact. So, how exactly can blockchain be leveraged for sustainable packaging, and what are the benefits it brings? Traditional packaging supply chains can be opaque, making it difficult for consumers to know the true environmental impact of the products they purchase. In an age where customers are increasingly interested in the sustainability credentials of the brands they support, blockchain offers a solution by providing a decentralised, immutable ledger that records every transaction made along the packaging supply chain. With blockchain, every step of a product's packaging journey can be tracked, from the sourcing of raw materials to manufacturing, distribution, and end-of-life disposal. This transparency allows consumers to verify claims about the sustainability of products, including the use of eco-friendly materials, recycling efforts, and carbon footprint reduction strategies. This level of traceability not only builds consumer trust but also helps companies adhere to regulations and certifications that require proof of sustainability, such as the Forest Stewardship Council (FSC) certification for paper products. According to a report from the Ellen MacArthur Foundation, over 80% of packaging is not recycled properly, leading to vast amounts of waste that pollute landfills and oceans. This is where blockchain can help drive change by promoting circularity — the process of reusing, recycling, and regenerating materials within the supply chain. By using smart contracts and tokens, businesses can reward customers who return packaging for reuse, further encouraging sustainable behaviour. Additionally, blockchain can assist in monitoring the effectiveness of recycling programs, ensuring that materials are returned and processed correctly, ultimately reducing the need for new resources. With these advanced tracking systems in place, businesses can make more informed decisions about the design of their packaging. For example, a company can identify packaging that is not being recycled effectively and shift towards more recyclable or compostable materials. Furthermore, blockchain allows for more efficient waste management practices, improving the ability to sort and process waste, which in turn reduces landfill usage. Companies often work with a multitude of suppliers, manufacturers, and logistics providers, all of whom may have different practices when it comes to sustainability. By creating a single, transparent ledger that all stakeholders can access, blockchain enables real-time sharing of data across the supply chain. This collaboration can lead to the sharing of best practices, the development of joint sustainability initiatives, and more consistent standards across industries. With verified data available to all parties, companies are held accountable for their sustainability claims, creating a more truthful and consistent approach to sustainable packaging. These could include packaging manufacturers, brands, recyclers, and even waste management organisations. By using blockchain to coordinate efforts, these diverse parties can work together more effectively, ensuring that packaging materials are sourced, used, and disposed of responsibly. This level of collaboration could result in more efficient systems that reduce waste and improve recycling rates across industries. The potential for blockchain to enhance transparency, reduce waste, and foster collaboration makes it a powerful tool for tackling the environmental challenges posed by traditional packaging methods. In the future, we may see widespread adoption of blockchain in the packaging industry, with companies and consumers alike benefiting from a more sustainable and circular system. As more organisations embrace this innovative approach, we can expect to see an acceleration in the development of new sustainable packaging solutions, which could ultimately lead to a reduction in waste, a decrease in the carbon footprint of packaging, and a cleaner, more sustainable environment for future generations. Ultimately, blockchain presents an exciting opportunity for revolutionising the packaging industry. By improving traceability, reducing waste, and fostering collaboration, blockchain can help businesses achieve their sustainability goals while providing consumers with greater confidence in the products they purchase. As the world continues to face pressing environmental challenges, blockchain for sustainable packaging could be a pivotal solution in the ongoing pursuit of a greener planet. Ethereum Stuck Between Retail Sell-Off And Whale Accumulation, Analyst Explains
Source: {'href': 'https://www.tradingview.com', 'title': 'TradingView'}    Published: 2025-05-09 08:00:37

According to a recent CryptoQuant Quicktake post by on-chain analyst BorisVest, Ethereum (ETH) appears to be stuck in a state of limbo. While retail investors are increasingly sending ETH to exchanges such as Binance – typically a sign of selling pressure – large investors are steadily withdrawing ETH from these platforms, indicating accumulation and long-term confidence. Optimism is building around the potential for a trend reversal, but on-chain data continues to deliver mixed signals regarding Ethereum's short- to medium-term direction. In his analysis, BorisVest highlighted that Ethereum metrics from Binance are sending ‘mixed signals.' Notably, mean exchange inflows have increased significantly since late 2024, suggesting growing sell pressure from retail traders. Similarly, mean exchange outflows have also been rising steadily since October 2023. However, these outflows are largely linked to whale wallets – addresses holding large amounts of ETH – implying that high-net-worth individuals are accumulating rather than selling. This divergence highlights a classic tug-of-war between retail fear and institutional confidence. He noted that during ETH's rally to $4,000 in early 2025, funding rates became overly positive as bullish sentiment took hold. This over-leveraged long positioning resulted in a sharp correction, driving ETH's price down to $1,400 by April. At present, funding rates are hovering in neutral territory, indicating a lack of clear leverage bias. BorisVest noted that if short interest rises and funding rates fall below zero, a short squeeze could ensue – potentially driving prices higher. Meanwhile, the taker buy/sell ratio, which tracks aggressive market orders, showed heavy selling pressure in late 2024 and early 2025 – right before Ethereum's steep decline. This ratio is now stabilizing, suggesting that sellers may be exhausted and buyers are gradually regaining strength. Although ETH is down 34.3% over the past year, several technical and on-chain indicators point toward a potential bullish trend reversal for the second-largest cryptocurrency by market cap. For instance, Ethereum recently flashed a golden cross on the daily chart, a bullish indicator that typically leads to major upward moves. Further, there are signs that the cryptocurrency may have already bottomed out for this market cycle.
