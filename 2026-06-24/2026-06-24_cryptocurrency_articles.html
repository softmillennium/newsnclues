In general terms, the threshold remains at 4.5 times the IPREM, which is equivalent to 37,800 euros gross per year per buyer, and is increased in certain provinces based on the estimated effort to access a home. The limit rises to 5.5 times the IPREM in Las Palmas, Navarra, Cantabria, Alicante, Cádiz, Pontevedra, Melilla, Ceuta, Valencia, and Zaragoza; it is set at up to 6.5 times the IPREM in Álava; and reaches up to 7.5 times the IPREM in Madrid, Barcelona, Girona, Málaga, Illes Balears, Santa Cruz de Tenerife, Bizkaia, and Gipuzkoa. Furthermore, the improvement factors for families with dependent children are maintained, so that the income cap is increased by 0.3 times the IPREM for each child and, in the case of single-parent families, it can be raised by an additional 70%. Likewise, the limit on net worth allowed for buyers is raised, from 100,000 to 150,000 euros. This measure is part of the package of actions promoted by the Executive to improve access to housing, in line with the objectives of the new State Housing Plan, combining policies to increase the supply of affordable housing with financing formulas such as these guarantees. The Guarantee Line is aimed at people with the capacity to take on a mortgage loan, but who do not have sufficient prior savings to cover the down payment needed to buy a home. The public backing can cover up to 20% of the loan, a percentage that can be extended up to 25% if the acquired home has an energy rating of D or higher. The guarantee has no cost for either buyers or credit institutions. Young people who wish to benefit from this measure must be, at most, 35 years old on the date of signing the mortgage loan. For families with dependent children, there is no age limit. The deadline for formalizing mortgage loans under this Line ends on December 31, 2027.
