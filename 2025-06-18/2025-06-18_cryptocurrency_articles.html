The Czech government on Wednesday survived a no-confidence vote triggered by a bitcoin scandal involving a drug dealer that has thrown the country's politics into turmoil. Some 98 out of 192 MPs present voted against an opposition motion to topple Prime Minister Petr Fiala's government — which could have triggered a snap ballot just four months before the country is scheduled to vote in regular elections. By contrast, 94 MPs voted to bring down the government. Vietnam Crypto Policy Signals A Bold Future As Nation Legalizes Crypto
Source: {'href': 'https://www.blockchainmagazine.net', 'title': 'Blockchain Magazine'}    Published: 2025-06-18 17:45:05

Vietnam has always been a leader in using crypto. But even though many people use it, the country's crypto market has been in a confusing state because the rules weren't clear. This is a big step forward to make crypto legal and set clear rules. Vietnam has just made a big decision with its new Law on Digital Technology Industry. It does not cover things like stocks, digital versions of traditional money, or other typical financial tools. This new clarity is great news for crypto businesses and investors who were unsure about the rules before. Importantly, it comes with strict cybersecurity and anti-money laundering (AML) requirements bringing Vietnam in line with global standards set by the Financial Action Task Force (FATF). Vietnam has been on FATF's “gray list” since 2023, and this law is a big step toward fixing that by tightening up rules to stop money laundering and terrorist financing. But this new law isn't only about crypto. It also offers big benefits to the entire digital tech industry. These benefits include tax breaks, incentives for land use, support for research and development, and even training programs to develop local skills. The aim is to make Vietnam a leader in new technologies like AI, semiconductors, and blockchain. This law isn't just a set of rules, it's Vietnam's way of declaring itself a leader in the global digital economy.
