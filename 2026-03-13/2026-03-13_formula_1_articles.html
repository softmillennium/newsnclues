'Confident' Gasly reflects on strong China Sprint Qualifying performance
Source: {'href': 'https://www.formula1.com', 'title': 'Formula 1'}    Published: 2026-03-13 13:06:39

TECH TALK – Winglets & weird wings: The best updates from China Pierre Gasly is targeting more points in Saturday's Sprint at the Chinese Grand Prix. The Frenchman managed to advance to the final part of Sprint Qualifying on Friday, posting a time that left him ahead of Max Verstappen – Gasly having also been cleared post-session of impeding the Red Bull driver. "I must say I'm extremely pleased with that, especially after last week," said Gasly, who finished 10th in Australia to claim the final point on offer at the opening round. "I think we found a lot more performance, good learning from Melbourne. I tried to put everything together today, and then I felt a lot better in the car straight from the first lap. Obviously graining was pretty bad for everyone [in practice] this morning, so it's going to be a nice challenge. Gasly's Alpine team mate Franco Colapinto managed to reach the second stage of Sprint Qualifying, but will start the Sprint from P16 after struggling to find enough performance. I didn't know the track and that made it a bit more difficult," he said. "I think the car was not in a bad place in FP1, but [it] just wouldn't do a step into Sprint Qualifying that I wanted. "The car was quick, Pierre showed that, he went through to SQ3, and that's positive for the team, but I need to understand a bit more on my side."

George Russell topped the timesheets for Mercedes in the only practice session of the Chinese Grand Prix weekend. The early Championship leader posted a best time of 1m 32.741s, with team mate Kimi Antonelli just over a tenth behind to make it a 1-2 for the Silver Arrows. The two McLarens followed, Lando Norris ahead of Oscar Piastri, over the Ferrari pair of Charles Leclerc and Lewis Hamilton. Ollie Bearman was an impressive seventh for Haas, as Max Verstappen, Nico Hulkenberg, and Pierre Gasly completed the top 10. Rookie Arvid Lindblad managed just six laps in his Racing Bulls machine before stopping on track with an issue, while Carlos Sainz was delayed in taking to the track with a data issue on his Williams.
