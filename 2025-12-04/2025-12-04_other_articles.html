Unlike The Winchesters, This Supernatural Prequel Would Have Lasted More Than One Season
Source: {'href': 'https://screenrant.com', 'title': 'Screen Rant'}    Published: 2025-12-04 17:00:00

Supernatural has rotten luck when it comes to spinoffs. The backdoor pilot for Supernatural: Bloodlines quite fairly didn't lead to a full series, the backdoor pilot for Wayward Sisters quite unfairly didn't lead to a full series, and when a spinoff did finally get off the ground with The Winchesters, it was canceled after a single season. At this point, it would be fair to ask whether Supernatural as a premise works without Sam and Dean Winchester. While that viewpoint certainly has merit, one could also argue that Supernatural hasn't always put its faith in the right spinoffs. Wayward Sisters had a lot of support behind it, but was never picked up, while The CW went ahead with The Winchesters despite confusing canon changes at its heart. Better Call Saul tied directly into Breaking Bad in an ingenious way that complemented both shows, whereas Yellowstone has thrived by making each release distinct. After years of trial and error, Supernatural apparently falls into a weird awkward gap between those two approaches. Bloodlines' city setting removed any trace of Supernatural from its DNA, whereas Wayward Sisters and The Winchesters were essentially Supernatural without Sam and Dean. While nothing ever came of it, Supernatural creator Eric Kripke once mentioned how the show's writers shared vague dreams of a Samuel Colt spinoff (via Starpulse), citing how Sam and Dean's story already felt like a modern-day western. The Old West vibe would have maintained the tone and feel of Supernatural's main show (unlike Bloodlines), but by virtue of being set 200 years earlier and featuring totally different characters, would feel a lot more unique than Wayward Sisters or The Winchesters. As a period piece, The Winchesters' 1970s timeline wasn't far enough from Sam and Dean's era to create a profound difference. Sure, the lack of cellphones helped, but the spinoff still enjoyed modern conveniences like cars and payphones, as well as a pop culture backdrop rooted in music and movies we still love today. Going back to the 1830s with Samuel Colt, viewers would have witnessed a very different society of monsters fighting a very different society of horse-riding hunters. With very little known about Colt and hunters generally in the 1800s, a prequel series would have reveled in its own corner of the Supernatural timeline almost entirely free from restrictions and potential lore contradictions. Having said that, Supernatural did lay some tantalizing groundwork for a larger story involving Samuel Colt. Exactly who this hunter was remains unclear, although Samuel himself is a possibility. And because two centuries separate the Winchester brothers from Samuel Colt, a Colt spinoff could introduce major new villains and monsters defeated long before the 21st century. Wayward Sisters and The Winchesters were both somewhat limited in that regard, with the latter's Akrida feeling too much like a rug-pull to succeed.
