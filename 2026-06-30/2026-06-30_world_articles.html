For 3,000 yuan ($443) a day, customers can hire an android to attract crowds at exhibitions, perform at events or even help stage a marriage proposal. But his venture has also revealed what viral videos of dancing, flipping Chinese robots have not: the heavily promoted technology is still years away from replacing human labor, whether on a factory floor or in a household. “The market for humanoid sales hasn't really taken off yet because today's robots still can't operate on their own – they're basically oversized toys,” he said. China already dominates the manufacturing and deployment of industrial robots, such as robotic arms in factories.
