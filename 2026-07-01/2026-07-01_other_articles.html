The International Academy of Astronautics has updated its recommendations for actions upon detecting extraterrestrial intelligence for the first time in 15 years. The International Academy of Astronautics (IAA) has updated its recommendations for actions in the event of detecting possible signs of extraterrestrial intelligence. For the first time in over 15 years, the document stipulates that any decision to send a response to a potential extraterrestrial civilization must be made only after international consultations. This is stated in the updated IAA declaration, reports UNN. According to the new principles, researchers who record a possible signal or other evidence of the existence of extraterrestrial intelligence must first thoroughly verify the discovery through independent observations. The updated recommendations also determine that no single state, organization, or scientific group should independently make a decision to transmit a message to a possible extraterrestrial civilization.
