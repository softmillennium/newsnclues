Fold Launches Nationwide Bitcoin Services Across All 50 States With BitGo
Source: {'href': 'https://bitcoinmagazine.com', 'title': 'Bitcoin Magazine'}    Published: 2025-12-18 18:54:18

Fold now offers Bitcoin services nationwide via its partnership with federally chartered BitGo Bank & Trust. Fold Holdings, Inc. ($FLD), a publicly traded Bitcoin financial services company, just announced that its platform is now available in all 50 U.S. states. The move marks a rare milestone in U.S. consumer Bitcoin services: Fold is the first platform to operate nationwide under a single federally supervised trust framework. Previously, state-by-state licensing and regulatory barriers constrained consumer access, particularly in states like New York. With BitGo's charter, Fold can now provide Bitcoin exchange and custody services across the entire country, including historically restrictive markets, this is according to the company's statement shared with Bitcoin Magazine. “BitGo B&T's federal bank charter combined with Fold's Bitcoin financial products gives the U.S. its first true national framework for Bitcoin access,” said CEO Will Reeves. Reeves emphasized that nationwide availability allows the company to scale its offerings and deliver Bitcoin products in line with federal oversight. BitGo provides the digital asset infrastructure through its Crypto-as-a-Service platform, enabling them to operate within a federally supervised compliance framework while continuing to innovate in rewards, payments, and custody services. Access has been limited by geography, but with a national framework, both companies can now operate as intended — responsibly and across the entire U.S.” This partnership positions FLD to capture a wider audience while aligning consumer crypto services with federal standards.

Marshall Islands Rolls Out Blockchain-Based UBI Using Stellar Network
Source: {'href': 'https://financefeeds.com', 'title': 'FinanceFeeds'}    Published: 2025-12-18 17:13:13

The program, locally named ENRA, began in November 2025 and delivers quarterly payments to eligible residents, providing essential financial services across the country's remote and geographically dispersed islands. This initiative coincides with growing institutional activity on Stellar, including pilot projects by major financial players exploring blockchain-based dollar tokenization. SDF issued a multimillion-dollar grant to facilitate USDM1's development and deployment, marking the first large-scale use of blockchain for government welfare. Citizens can receive their quarterly payments either through traditional banking methods or directly via USDM1 tokens in Lomalo, a mobile app purpose‑built for RMI and powered by Crossmint wallets on Stellar. Denelle Dixon, CEO and Executive Director of SDF, said Every day, people can receive and spend money onchain, and get access to basic financial services that they have been shut out of in the traditional financial system.” Previously, UBI and other government payments relied on cash deliveries via official transport vessels, which were slow, costly, and limited in reach. With USDM1 and Lomalo, citizens in remote atolls can now receive their funds within seconds, bypassing traditional logistical challenges. Funding for the UBI program is tied in part to the country's sovereign trust arrangements, including compensation-linked funds connected to historical U.S. nuclear testing. While early reactions highlight the efficiency and auditability of on-chain payments, the government acknowledges ongoing challenges, including digital literacy, smartphone access, and connectivity gaps in remote areas. Still, the Marshall Islands' experiment is being closely watched as a real-world test of whether public blockchains like Stellar can reliably support large-scale government welfare programs.
