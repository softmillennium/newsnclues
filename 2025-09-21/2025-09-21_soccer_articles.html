Wrexham's first league victory over Norwich City being broadcast live on TV in the United States should have surprised no one. Throw in the extra intrigue of USMNT striker Josh Sargent leading the Norwich attack and it really was a no-brainer for Paramount+ to make the game one of their three 3pm kick-offs shown live on Saturday. It was one they passed as a tweak of formation, with two debutants in Issa Kabore and Dominic Hyam starting in a re-jigged back five, led to Wrexham deservedly prevailing 3-2. “Football is about trying to be secure at one end and keep the attacking threat that we'd shown so far this season. But the defence also looked much more secure than it has all season, even allowing for the two lapses in concentration that were punished ruthlessly by Jack Stacey and Jovon Makama. Not only did Kabore provide two stunning assists from the right flank — Windass and Longman the beneficiaries — but there was a cohesion to Wrexham's defensive efforts that had been totally lacking against QPR a week earlier. Having been recalled to Mauricio Pochettino's USMNT squad earlier this month for the friendly double-header against South Korea and Japan, the Norwich captain is eyeing a place at next year's World Cup. A glance at Sargent's metrics for Norwich points towards someone who makes the most of any service that comes his way. Prior to Wrexham's visit, his five goals had come from 121 touches of the ball spread across five games, according to Opta. Those five goals had come from a mere 16 shots this season. Moore, by way of contrast, had four goals from the same number of shots but he'd been much more involved, chalking up 195 touches including 11 headed clearances. Against Wrexham, Sargent saw slightly more of the ball. And yet his three attempts came with a combined expected goals (xG) of just 0.09, underling what a good job Hyam and his team-mates did on the U.S. striker. Otherwise, it was an afternoon to forget for Norwich's talisman, who turned down a big-money move to Bundesliga side Wolfsburg during the summer. He had 48 touches (only Cleworth with 66 and Ben Sheaf with 61 managed more for the visitors), sent over nine crosses (six more than anyone else in red) and managed two headed clearances to go with his assists for Windass and Longman. “The medical team were behind me and saying, ‘His sprint distances are completely off the scale, can we get him off?' I want people to understand how far we have come.”


World Cup 2026 Drives Revolutionary Payment Upgrades in Mexico to Secure Economic Gains From Global Tourism Surge
Source: {'href': 'https://www.travelandtourworld.com', 'title': 'Travel And Tour World'}    Published: 2025-09-21 00:57:49

With an estimated 5.5 million fans expected, seamless, secure, and modern payment infrastructure has become critical for converting international visitor spending into substantial economic gains. The higher average spending of foreign tourists—nearly three times that of local consumers—makes efficient payment processing a strategic necessity across retail, hospitality, entertainment, and food and beverage sectors.The push for modernization is driven not only by transaction volume but also by the diverse payment preferences of global travelers, including credit cards, digital wallets, and contactless options. Streamlined systems will allow businesses to scale operations during peak periods, ensure frictionless transactions at hotels, stadiums, restaurants, and retail outlets, and enhance overall visitor experience. By investing in advanced, agile payment platforms, Mexico aims to transform the influx of international fans into measurable economic growth while positioning its tourism and hospitality sectors for long-term success.Advertisement Recent data on consumer spending patterns underscores the urgency. International tourists, though they represent a relatively small portion of total transactions, contribute disproportionately to overall spending. On average, foreign visitors spend almost three times more per purchase than local consumers. This significant difference in transaction values illustrates the need for payment systems capable of efficiently handling larger, high-value purchases while minimizing friction.For businesses operating in Mexico during the World Cup, upgrading payment infrastructure is no longer a luxury. Modern payment platforms that combine agility, security, and global compatibility are poised to become strategic enablers of revenue growth rather than mere conveniences.Advertisement The sports and entertainment sector, in particular, stands to benefit from comprehensive payment integration. From ticket purchases to in-stadium concessions, smooth and reliable payment experiences can significantly enhance customer satisfaction while increasing operational efficiency. Efficient checkout processes reduce queues and transaction bottlenecks, especially during peak times, ensuring that fans enjoy a seamless experience while venues maximize revenue opportunities.Similarly, the food and beverage industry can leverage advanced payment technology to handle surges in demand efficiently. Faster and frictionless payments not only improve customer satisfaction but also increase the number of transactions handled per hour, directly contributing to higher revenues during high-traffic events like the World Cup.Advertisement Beyond transactional efficiency, the broader tourism and hospitality sectors are beginning to view digital payment experiences as a critical component of service quality. Hotels, restaurants, and tour operators that adopt global payment solutions and contactless technologies are likely to gain a competitive advantage, ensuring that visitors enjoy a hassle-free experience from arrival to departure.Looking forward, businesses preparing for the World Cup must focus on three key pillars of payment modernization:Diversifying payment methods to accommodate international visitor preferences, including credit cards, digital wallets, and other emerging payment technologies.Minimizing friction at checkout to prevent cart abandonment, reduce queues, and speed up transactions across all points of sale.Delivering personalized payment experiences that foster customer loyalty and encourage repeat business beyond the duration of the tournament.The upcoming World Cup presents Mexico with a unique economic opportunity. Effective payment systems can unlock higher spending from international visitors, optimize operations across multiple sectors, and enhance the overall visitor experience.
