Yet, according to the Index, between June and October, Chelsea recorded 23 injuries ‚Äì more than any of the nine participating clubs from Europe's top leagues. While Chelsea, who played 64 games across the 2024-25 season, saw their injury count rise, the Index, compiled by global insurance firm Howden, noted an overall decrease in injuries among the top European clubs. "In principle you would expect this increased workload to lead to an increase in the number of injuries sustained, as a possible rise in overall injury severity," the Index report stated, but concluded: "The data would suggest a minimal impact on overall injury figures." The nine teams from Europe's top leagues ‚Äì England, Spain, Italy, Germany, and France ‚Äì recorded 146 injuries from June to October, a reduction from 174 in the previous year.
