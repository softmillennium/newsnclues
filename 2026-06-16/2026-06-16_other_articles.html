Fairborn UFO Conference expands to meet growing national fascination
Source: {'href': 'https://www.daytondailynews.com', 'title': 'Dayton Daily News'}    Published: 2026-06-16 08:00:00

A local UFO informational event is gathering national attention as fascination in Unidentified Flying Objects, or UFOs, is growing. It all started last year when the Fairborn UFO Conference was presented by a local club, UFO 101, that meets monthly in the New Carlisle Library. The list of speakers, presentations and vendors has increased to include speakers well known to the UFO community. One of the organizers, New Carlisle resident David McWhorter, is amazed at how the event has grown exponentially in just one year. “It's not just local now – they are coming from all over the country,” said McWhorter, who has sold tickets to attendees from Washington, D.C., Chicago and New York. The event is not just about UFOs or Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena, UAPs, which are not just in the air. It's also about the natural wonder we humans have when we see or hear about something that just doesn't seem right. People want to know what is going on. Living near and working at Wright-Patterson AFB has fueled local interest in UFOs for decades. Those local connections will be explored extensively in this event. The conference will run all day on July 11 from 8 a.m. to 7 p.m. Speakers will focus on recent UFO evidence, Roswell, the infamous Project Blue Book, Wright-Patterson connections, crop circles and even Steven Spielberg's recently released movie “Disclosure Day.” Vendors will be open all day. Well-known nationally, Sheehan is a disclosure attorney exposing UFO and UAP government cover-ups. Bestselling author Donald Schmitt will present a Deep Dive into the Roswell Incident. Jeff Wilson will discuss those enigmatic crop circles. MUFON, which stands for the Mutual UFO Network, self identifies as the leading authority for reporting and investigating sightings, abductions and other unexplained phenomena. Experiencers, or people who have had a UFO or UAP encounter, will be able to share their personal stories with others in a special area. The day will conclude with a much-anticipated question and answer session. This event will be preceded on Friday evening by a VIP reception from 7-9 p.m. Appetizers, private meet-and-greet with speakers and conference admission is included in the VIP ticket. If all tickets are not sold in advance, there may be same-day tickets available at the door July 11. General conference admission advanced tickets are $33.85, including a purchase fee. VIP Tickets are $65.78, including the fee, and must be purchased in advance. Organizers hope that this conference gives all who attend – UFO believers and skeptics – an opportunity to open their minds to possibilities. Texas Couple Indicted in Alleged Multi-Million Dollar “Psychic Fraud” Scheme, FBI Says
Source: {'href': 'https://www.easttexasbanner.com', 'title': 'easttexasbanner.com'}    Published: 2026-06-16 04:31:00

FRISCO, Texas — A North Texas couple has been indicted in what federal investigators describe as a years-long “psychic fraud” scheme that allegedly targeted emotionally vulnerable individuals and stole more than $2.5 million from victims seeking help with relationships, grief, and personal struggles. According to the FBI and the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Western District of Washington, Bridgette Doreen Evans, 47, also known as “Jolene Travis,” and her partner, Vinnie John Uwanawich, 44, both of Frisco, Texas, were arrested last week following a federal indictment alleging conspiracy to commit wire and mail fraud, multiple counts of wire fraud, and multiple counts of mail fraud. Federal authorities say the couple preyed upon emotionally distressed individuals by posing as psychics capable of removing curses, fixing romantic relationships, and cleansing “bad energy.” Investigators allege Evans used fake identities, including “Jolene Travis,” “Joy John,” and “Joy Paige,” to advertise psychic services through social media platforms, targeting people experiencing divorce, grief, loneliness, or relationship issues. Prosecutors say Evans would ask victims to provide detailed information about their assets before allegedly persuading them to liquidate accounts, purchase gold coins, take out loans, or send cash for supposed “cleansing rituals.” Authorities say another victim sent approximately $86,000, while a third victim allegedly lost more than $258,000, including being convinced to take out loans for a $125,000 Chevrolet Corvette and allow access to credit cards to pay off alleged “karmic debts.” Victims were allegedly told their bad luck, failed relationships, or emotional struggles were caused by curses that could only be removed through expensive financial sacrifices. Authorities also allege Uwanawich helped facilitate the scheme by managing accounts that received victim payments, selling gold coins, moving money, and vouching for Evans' legitimacy to victims. The indictment further claims Evans allegedly concealed her real identity because of prior psychic fraud convictions in both Florida and Texas. During a period when Evans was reportedly incarcerated in Florida on an unrelated psychic fraud case, investigators say co-conspirators allegedly continued operating under the “Jolene Travis” persona to keep the scheme going. “This case is yet another demonstration of how fraudsters exploit vulnerable situations to gain their victims' trust,” said W. Mystery 'potato-shaped' figure hovering over Cheyenne Mountain reported in latest UFO files
Source: {'href': 'https://krdo.com', 'title': 'KRDO'}    Published: 2026-06-16 03:41:19

COLORADO SPRINGS, Colo. (KRDO) -- A mysterious figure described as "potato-shaped" hovering over Colorado Springs is reported in the latest UFO files. Five U.S. Army service members reported seeing the object spotted over Cheyenne Mountain on Feb. 15, 2022, while near Fort Carson. They described it as large, oddly shaped, and shimmering white. After reviewing the incident, government analysts believe the sighting may have been caused by sunlight reflecting off snow-covered ground and nearby clouds. However, investigators said they have low confidence in that conclusion because they could not be certain about the weather conditions, snow cover or each witness's exact viewpoint. Investigators noted that no airplanes or balloons were known to be operating near Cheyenne Mountain at the time. The witness also said the object disappeared almost instantly after about two minutes, making it seem as though it had "cloaked" rather than flown away. KRDO13 spoke with the Director of the Mutual UFO Network, Seth Feinstein, who says they use a point system to rate credibility for reports, and this sighting earns a few. I don't think it was the lens flare or reflection of the snow, but, you know, I've seen so many cases of clouds that people would swear were UAPs," says Feinstein. 'Supernatural Ops Team' Launches Major 'Divine Beast' Themed Content Update
Source: {'href': 'https://www.invenglobal.com', 'title': 'Inven Global'}    Published: 2026-06-16 03:15:43

Giant Games announced on the 16th that it has released a major content update for its multiplayer cooperative party horror game, 'Supernatural Ops Team,' centered around a 'Divine Beast' theme. First, Vivian's new Phantasm Dual costume, 'Legacy of the Deep Sea,' has been unveiled. Designed with a deep-sea concept, this costume features exclusive animations and customization elements. This feature offers a variety of collectible rewards and customization items, including Muryeong-ah's limited-edition Stella costume 'Nine-Tailed Fox,' Wol's limited-edition Legend costume 'Dragon Horse,' as well as profile customization items, chat bubbles, profile frames, and accessories. A significant number of equipment skins featuring exclusive motions and special effects have also been added. Players can discover various items reflecting the Divine Beast theme, such as 'Poseidon,' 'Northern Qilin,' 'Silver Scales of the Lake,' 'Ghost Vine,' and 'Flower Blooming at Night,' with some items featuring dedicated motions, special effects, and sound design. Reward content for both new and returning players has also been prepared. By completing in-game missions, players can obtain Ray's new 'Whisper of the Fairy Tale' costume for free, along with additional rewards such as Souls, various growth resources, and customization items. Collecting specific limited-edition costumes grants additional rewards, and players who complete all collection goals will receive exclusive rewards, including the special 'Divine Beast Collector' title and an animated profile frame. Limited-time recharge rewards also offer various benefits, such as new costumes, profile customization items, and Agent Selection Packages. A representative for "Supernatural Ops Team" stated, "This update goes beyond just adding new costumes; it is a major content expansion that enhances the fun of collecting, growing, and customizing. We hope players enjoy a richer gameplay experience through these diverse costume concepts and rewards."
