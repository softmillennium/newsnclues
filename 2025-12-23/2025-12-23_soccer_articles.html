World Cup Fan Fests will feature 'Freedom' trailer-trucks Jason Smith / Getty Images A White House task force facilitated a proposal for World Cup host cities in the United States to house vast ‘Freedom' trailer-trucks with United States flags at official Fan Festivals in celebration of the country's 250th anniversary. Trump appointed Andrew Giuliani, the son of former New York City mayor Rudy Giuliani, to run its day-to-day operations as executive director. The task force's primary role is to lead and coordinate federal efforts in support of the tournament, notably across safety and security concerns. The task force holds regular weekly calls with the 11 American host city committees, which are effectively subsidiaries of FIFA's U.S. operations. At one recent meeting, the task force invited a pitch from organizers of America's 250th birthday celebrations, which falls on July 4, 2026, at the start of the World Cup's round of 16. Major FIFA Fan Fests have already been announced by several host cities across the United States, such as Liberty State Park in Jersey City, and a 25,000-person capacity Fan Fest at the National WW1 Museum and Memorial in Kansas City, Missouri. Others, such as the San Francisco Bay Area, are considering a reduced slate of Fan Fests amid the major financial burden FIFA has transferred onto cities. Since his January inauguration, Trump has taken several steps to ramp up celebrations for ‘America 250.' This included the establishment of the White House Task Force 250, which is also chaired by President Trump and includes representation from cabinet members. He further announced that between June 25 and July 10, the U.S. will hold a Great American State Fair on the National Mall, “celebrating America's exceptional history and culture.” He also announced a national prayer event, “to rededicate (America) as one nation under God”, as well as a UFC event at the White House and the inaugural Patriot Games — a four-day athletic event involving one male and one female high school athlete from each U.S. state and territory. Previously, another organization, named America250, was created by Congress in 2016, charged with organizing the national commemoration, and this has bipartisan representation from Congress, as well as honorary presidency positions for former presidents Barack Obama and George W. Bush, and First Ladies Michelle Obama and Laura Bush. The Atlantic reported that America250 had launched a partnership with Moms For Liberty, a conservative group, while also securing sponsorships with companies such as Palantir, UFC, Coinbase and Oracle, whose executives have financially supported Trump. According to four people with knowledge of the call, all of whom wished to remain anonymous because they were not authorized to speak publicly on the matter, these were described as mobile museum freedom trucks and airstream trailers, which would tell the story of America's history and independence. The news release added: “At a time when 64 percent of Americans cannot pass the citizenship test, these key exhibits aim to make our nation's history more accessible and reinforce patriotism among Americans of all ages.” The World Cup task force declined to comment, but senior sources with knowledge of the presentation confirmed the offer was true, while saying it is an offering rather than an obligation for host cities. Other offers included the provision of U.S. swag and tourism guides. The task force did not confirm which specific 250-themed entity set out the offer at the World Cup meeting, but indicated there is internal government involvement, as well as public/private partnerships working across America 250 initiatives. In a recent interview with The Athletic, Giuliani described the World Cup as “an incredible opportunity to show off” America's first 250 years. He added: “President Trump, more than anything, represents the fact that, as Americans, we don't have to apologize anymore for whatever imperfections may exist in our country's history. We can look at the overall work of the last 250 years and what America has done for Western civilization, and truly celebrate that American greatness.” White House spokesman Davis Ingle said the Trump Administration is “committed to working with and providing resources and opportunities with all of our partners, including the FIFA World Cup 2026, to deliver one of the most iconic sporting experiences in history for Americans and the world to enjoy.” FIFA has already demonstrated willingness to promote Trump's agenda, most famously when awarding the U.S. president its inaugural Peace Prize this month.
