Back in 2021, AMD's crypto-mining-focused BC-250 graphics card, powered by the Sony PlayStation 5's APU instead of regular GPU chips, started popping up on eBay for around $500. The unit also included a standard 8-pin PCIe connector to feed power and an I/O shield. To power up the board, a standard 1,000W PSU was used along with an SSD, peripherals, monitor, CMOS battery, and a standard 120mm fan to actively cool the heatsink.
