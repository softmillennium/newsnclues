Google will require developer verification for Android apps outside the Play Store
Source: {'href': 'https://techcrunch.com', 'title': 'TechCrunch'}    Published: 2025-08-25 18:23:12

Google is tightening security measures around Android app distribution, the company announced on Monday. The tech giant stresses that this does not mean developers can't distribute outside of the Play Store through other app stores or via sideloading — Android will remain open in that regard. However, developers who appreciated the anonymity of alternative distribution methods will no longer have that option. According to its own survey, Google says that more than 50 times more malware came through internet-sideloaded sources compared with Google Play, where it has required developer verification since 2023. By September 2026, any app installed on an Android device in Brazil, Indonesia, Singapore, and Thailand will have to meet the new requirements. Developers will have to provide their legal name, address, email, and phone number, which could push independent developers to register as a business for their own privacy's sake. Google notes that student and hobbyist developers will be able to use a separate type of Android Developer Console account when this system rolls out, as their needs differ from commercial developers. Porting games from x86 to ARM isn't necessarily a massive undertaking, but it shows how much of a specific platform the Switch 2 can be. This radio station, dubbed UVB-76, has spent much of 2025 broadcasting cryptic messages, strange music, and pirate interruptions. Because shortwave broadcasts can traverse huge distances, it became a favorite medium for soldiers and spies. Surya was trained with nine years of data collected by NASA's Solar Dynamics Observatory (SDO), an instrument that has orbited the sun since 2010, taking high-resolution images every 12 seconds.
