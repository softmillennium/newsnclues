‘I Know What You Did Last Summer' Slashes Sydney Sweeney's Supernatural Horror Hit at Global Box Office
Source: {'href': 'https://collider.com', 'title': 'Collider'}    Published: 2025-07-24 14:01:00

It's been a tough year so far for studio horror films, with the odd exception, of course. Only a few weeks after Sony's 28 Years Later appeared to defy trends and deliver a respectable haul at the box office, the studio's I Know What You Did Last Summer legacy sequel found itself being overwhelmed by competition. The slasher film debuted this past week, and underperformed with just under $15 million in its opening frame at the domestic box office. Audiences were clearly more interested in watching the holdover hits Superman, Jurassic World Rebirth, and F1. However, despite its lukewarm debut, I Know What You Did Last Summer has now overtaken one of last year's surprise horror hits, the low-budget supernatural chiller Immaculate. Starring Sydney Sweeney as a nun who moves to Italy and discovers horrific secrets at her convent, Immaculate concluded its box office run with $35 million worldwide and $15 million domestically. It served as Sweeney's first theatrical release following the massive success of the romantic comedy Anyone But You, in which she starred alongside Glen Powell. In the same year, Sweeney would also appear in the superhero film Madame Web, which tanked both critically and commercially. The movie holds a 71% score on the aggregator website Rotten Tomatoes, but its audience score is resting at a far lower 58%. The critics' consensus on RT reads, "Immaculate in conception if not always in execution, this religiously themed horror outing is saved by a divine performance from Sydney Sweeney." Directed by Jennifer Kaytin Robinson, I Know What You Did Last Summer has overtaken Immaculate's $15 million domestic haul in five days of release. The movie's global haul stands at $27 million so far. It opened to lackluster reviews, and is currently sitting at a 38% score on the aggregator website Rotten Tomatoes, where the critics' consensus reads, "Faithfully harkening back to the original for better or worse, this I Know What You Did Last Summer reboot serves up plenty of nostalgia without finding a compelling hook of its own."
