Paris Court of Appeal upholds decision in dispute between French Tennis Federation and Viagogo
Source: {'href': 'https://www.theticketingbusiness.com', 'title': 'The Ticketing Business'}    Published: 2025-07-24 13:49:19

The Paris Court of Appeal has confirmed the violation by Viagogo and Viagogo Entertainment, which illegally sold tickets to the Rolex Paris Masters. The French Tennis Federation (FFT), which has the sole right to select which companies sell tickets for the event, expressed its satisfaction with this latest development, confirming the judgement that was reached on November 8, 2022 by the Paris Judicial Court. The FFT has had a long-running dispute with Viagogo over the resale of tickets for its events, including the French Open at Roland-Garros. Judges recalled in their most recent decision that any form of economic activity, the purpose of which is to generate a profit, and which would not exist if the sporting event was not held, must be considered as exploitation within the meaning of Article L.333-1 of the Sports Code. In this case, generating a profit was done so without the permission of the FFT. The Paris Court of Appeal specified that the body's monopoly covers all ticketing rights, whether tickets are sold on the primary or secondary market. The FFT reminded tennis fans that tickets for the Paris Masters are only available through the official Paris Masters website, or FFT-approved retailers.
