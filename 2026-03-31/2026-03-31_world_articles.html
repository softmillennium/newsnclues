Source: {'href': 'https://www.theglobeandmail.com', 'title': 'The Globe and Mail'}    Published: 2026-03-31 16:28:28

Mr. Lewis, 88, passed away in Toronto on Tuesday, just days after his son, Avi, was elected leader of the federal NDP. He had been battling cancer and was in a hospice, according to his sister, Janet Solberg. A statement issued by the federal party said Mr. Lewis's wife, Michele; his daughters, Ilana and Jenny; and Ms. Solberg, were with him at the end. “The world has lost a voice of unmatched eloquence and integrity.” Mr. Lewis, the son of former federal NDP leader David Lewis, led the Ontario NDP from 1970 to 1978, serving as official opposition leader from 1975 to 1977. “But he is still with us and hanging on to see the next chapter of our movement,” Mr. Lewis told party members after his win. “At age 88, he is more passionate about the promise of democratic socialism than he has ever been in his life,” Avi Lewis said. After his career in provincial politics, Stephen Lewis was appointed Canada's ambassador to the United Nations in 1984 while Brian Mulroney was prime minister. But his association with the UN did not end then. He served until 1991 as a special adviser to the UN Secretary General on Africa. And from 2001 to 2006, he was the United Nations Special Envoy for HIV/AIDS in Africa. With his daughter Ilana Landsberg-Lewis, Mr. Lewis co-founded the Stephen Lewis Foundation to work with people in Africa suffering from and affected by HIV/AIDS.
