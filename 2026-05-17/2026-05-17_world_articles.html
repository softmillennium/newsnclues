A laboratory-confirmed case was also reported in the DRC capital, Kinshasa, from a person returning from Ituri, the WHO said. Bundibugyo virus-disease contacts or cases should not travel internationally, unless as part of a medical evacuation, the WHO said. The agency advised immediately isolating confirmed cases and monitoring contacts daily, with restricted national travel and no international travel until 21 days after exposure. At the same time, the WHO urged countries not to close their borders or restrict travel and trade out of fear, as this could lead to people and goods making informal border crossings that are not monitored. The DRC's dense tropical forests are a natural reservoir for the Ebola virus. BEIRUT, May 17. /TASS/. Hezbollah fighters carried out 19 armed operations against Israeli forces in the past day in response to enemy ceasefire violations, the Shia movement said in a statement. "Islamic resistance units carried out 19 operations against the occupation forces active in Lebanon," the statement reads. "In particular, drones attacked a command post in the city of Bayada and barracks in the settlement of Yaara," Hezbollah specified. In addition, Hezbollah fighters ambushed an Israeli mechanized convoy in the Rashaf municipality. "Several mine blasts inflicted troop and equipment losses on enemy forces," the statement said. Moreover, Hezbollah units also hit an Israeli Merkava tank and knocked out bulldozers demolishing homes in the Deir Seryan and Hadath municipalities.
