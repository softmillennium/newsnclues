An operating system, such as Microsoft Windows or Apple iOS, is the program responsible for managing every other application on a computer. However, most quantum computers are designed and built for a specific function; for example, to run an experiment or simulation. This limits the potential functionality of quantum computers and hampers their connectivity. But on March 12, scientists published a new study in Nature describing QNodeOS, an operating system for quantum computers that works with all kinds of machines irrespective of the type of qubits they use. Such an operating system would enable multiple quantum computers to be connected together and controlled by the same central platform.
