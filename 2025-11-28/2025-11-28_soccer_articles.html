Before the draw, teams will be seeded and divided into four pots based on their FIFA rankings as of November/December 2025. Teams will be randomly drawn from each pot and assigned to one of the 12 groups. However, teams from the same confederation cannot meet — except for UEFA. There can be up to two European teams per group. With the first game of the 2026 World Cup taking place at Estadio Azteca in Mexico City on June 11, Mexico have automatically been assigned to Group A. Sixteen teams are in UEFA's play-offs, with four-time winners Italy needing to overcome Northern Ireland and then Wales or Bosnia and Herzegovina to reach next year's tournament. All qualifiers will be placed into pot four, leading to some potentially tricky draws, such as four-time winners Italy, were they to progress. Four nations have qualified for their first World Cup.
