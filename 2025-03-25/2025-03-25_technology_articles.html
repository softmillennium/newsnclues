We’ve outsourced our confirmation biases to search engines
Source: arstechnica    Published: 2025-03-25 19:41:11

People are often selective about the information they accept, seeking out sources that confirm their biases while discounting those that challenge their beliefs. Search engines could potentially change this by prioritizing results from high-quality, credible sources, but people have learned to game the algorithms used by search engines. The study found that people tend to devise search terms that are specific enough to ensure that the results will end up reinforcing their existing beliefs. Invisible swapping of search terms to something more general can go a long way toward enabling people to change their mind. The researchers, Eugina Leung and Oleg Urminsky, focused on a simple question: is caffeine good or bad for you? They found that using neutral search terms like "what are the health effects of caffeine" would get a mixture of pros and cons.

The Pentagon Sent Out a Warning Against Using Signal Right Before Yemen Group Chat Fiasco
Source: gizmodo    Published: 2025-03-25 19:24:36

The Pentagon warned against using Signal in a memo just last week, according to NPR. The memo was delivered department-wide days before The Atlantic published a bombshell revelation that its editor-in-chief Jeffrey Goldberg had been erroneously added to a Signal group chat that involved detailed discussions of planned strikes on Houthi rebels in Yemen. Members in the Signal chat included U.S. Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth and Vice President J.D. Vance, among others. The Pentagon wrote in its memo, “Russian professional hacking groups are employing the ‘linked devices’ features to spy on encrypted conversations.” A linked device allows users to sign into their account from multiple places, and incoming and outgoing messages appear on all devices. Because Signal is end-to-end encrypted and messages are only stored locally on devices with an encryption key, hackers can create malicious phishing pages or QR codes associated with group chats, which users can send to one another as invites. The Pentagon's memo states that after gaining access to the malicious code, groups can add their own device as a linked device, allowing them to view every message sent by the unwitting user in real time, bypassing the end-to-end encryption. Signal has defended its security practices and outlined the great lengths it has gone to prevent itself from being able to access user data. But at the end of the day, neither Signal nor end-to-end encryption technology is going to protect someone sharing classified war plans in an open group chat. The Atlantic's editor-in-chief Jeffrey Goldberg was involved in a Signal group chat that included discussions of planned strikes on Houthi rebels in Yemen. White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt has been on the defensive trying to put out this latest fire, posting on X again on Tuesday to say that no war plans or otherwise classified materials were discussed in the chat. An investigation into how a journalist was added to the conversation remains ongoing.

Some of the improvements Goh noted include “binding,” which refers to how well AI image generators maintain correct relationships between attributes and objects; a model with poor binding might get a prompt for a blue star plus a red triangle and create a red star and no triangle. Most image models struggle with this, Goh said, often mixing up colors and shapes when asked to render multiple items — typically around 5 to 8. He says this new image generation tool can correctly bind attributes for 15 to 20 objects without confusion, representing a significant improvement in accuracy and reliability. An example of Images in ChatGPT’s “binding” capabilities. OpenAI Users will also notice an improvement in text rendering, which makes it easier to generate coherent text without typos on an image (in existing tools, you’ll often notice that text gets garbled pretty easily). Getting text rendering right was a significant challenge, Goh said. If small titles or text elements have typos or errors, the entire image can become unusable. “This was just like a process of iteration that took many, many months to get right,” Goh said. While not perfect, he said the team reached a point where the text quality is consistently usable (where it tends to blunder is really small text). “It’s been just many months of small improvements.”
