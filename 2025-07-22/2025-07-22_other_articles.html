In many ways, the story of contemporary Ireland is encapsulated in the astonishing growth in the share of overall tax revenue contributed by corporation tax. In 1984, it was just 4 per cent. By 2014, it had almost tripled to 11 per cent. It has by now almost tripled again to 29 per cent. Most of this dizzying increase is what official bodies call “excess”. “In other words,” as the Irish Fiscal Advisory Council puts it, “it cannot be explained by underlying domestic activity.” Or, in the Central Bank's formulation, it is “disconnected from actual economic activity in Ireland”. It is paranormal and, I think, unprecedented. Has any state ever driven such a great distance on the fumes of airy superfluity? To get a sense of the scale of this ectoplasmic largesse, we can ask: how much money would the State have taken in last year if the share of its revenues accounted for by corporation tax were the same as 2014? So we're looking at €16 billion of last year's revenue that was accounted for simply by the magical growth in the proportional contribution of corporation tax paid by a tiny number of American multinationals. To put that in context, €16 billion is the combined budget of the departments of Education and Transport this year. DEAR READERS: I was recently interviewed by the Nashville Voyager Magazine. I thought I would share some of the questions they asked me, so you would also get to know a little bit more about me. I have been writing this column for many years, and I am so proud to offer the topic of spirituality in a non-scary, non-woo-woo way, and I never dreamed my column would be as popular or welcomed as it has been, and I am so grateful to each of you who takes the time to read my words each week. Q: Bonnie, please kick things off for us with an introduction to yourself and your story. For many generations, my ancestors have had this gift from God running through their veins. The house I grew up in was an old historical schoolhouse, and there always seemed to be spirits available for my viewing and engaging with. Since my mother had the gift of mediumship [and] could see what I was seeing and could alleviate any fears that I had with connecting to the spirit world, I grew up thinking everyone saw spirits. As I grew older, the spirit world would become even stronger in my life. Hearing the voice of God asking me to do this work, I asked, “Why?” when he said, “Little One, the world is becoming darker and darker, and we need more light.” I closed down my Salon I owned and “Messages from Heaven” was born. For over a decade, I have been a full-time demonstrating medium, giving audiences, large and small, a glimpse into Heaven with the intention of providing proof that life goes on and the bond of love can never die. I am now at the point of opening a private office just fifteen minutes from Nashville in Franklin, Tenn. I have also written three books found on Amazon and I've been writing a weekly column for the last seven years called “Ask the Psychic” as a freelance columnist for [MediaNews Group], which is seen in many of their papers. Q: I'm sure it wasn't obstacle-free, but would you say the journey has been fairly smooth so far? I am not going to force a belief on anyone, I am just here to show by giving evidence that life goes on and there is another way to look at death and living.
