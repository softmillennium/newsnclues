Rosenior is regarded as a bright young coach but struggled to win over his new squad and quickly lost momentum after an encouraging start. Player power bubbled to the surface, culminating in the Englishman being fired 107 days into a six-and-a-half-year deal, and the lack of leadership in a young dressing room has been exposed. Rosenior never recovered from Fernández, who has not exactly been discreet about his interest in joining Real Madrid, being hit with an internal two-game ban. European wins over the two Madrid clubs, Inter and Marseille were reminders of the levels Liverpool can reach. Chelsea: João Pedro has impressed since joining from Brighton, scoring 23 goals. Estêvão Willian, the young Brazil winger, showed promise before a hamstring injury ended his campaign. Liverpool: Many Liverpool fans would give a blunt answer with patience in Slot wearing thin and Xabi Alonso available. A goalkeeper may also be required should Alisson join the exodus of experience. Chelsea: Get the next manager right and empower him. Listen to him on recruitment and find someone who will get rid of the entitlement and laziness in the dressing room. That means looking in the mirror, being honest and working out whether anything in the structure has to change. One thing's for sure: they have to buy some experienced players.

More than 400,000 square feet of gyms, locker rooms, meeting rooms and community spaces are combined with 17 playing spaces, including two regulation-size indoor fields. The facility shares its name with Atlanta billionaire Arthur M. Blank, of Home Depot success, who was joined by investments from iconic Atlanta-based brands like Coca-Cola and Chick-fil-A, among others. The massive, 123-acre development includes 17 outdoor playing surfaces, 13 regulation natural grass fields, two artificial turf fields and two sand pitches for beach soccer. There are also classrooms, meeting spaces, dining and more inside the facility. The facility is expected to bring in $107 million annually, beginning in 2027, with $2.9 million going toward state and local taxes. More than 350 employees will work from the site, and the facility anticipates hosting more than 215,000 visitors each year. After making his fortune through the co-founding of Home Depot, Arthur Blank has spent much of his time (and money) investing in professional sports in Atlanta and philanthropic endeavors. "My journey with soccer goes back to when Joshua, my son ... (was) playing soccer at five years old at a very competitive level," Blank said during the opening ceremonies of the new facility that shares his name. Blank later met the commissioner of Major League Soccer, Don Garber, and the two worked to bring a professional team to Atlanta. In 2014, the MLS awarded an expansion team in Atlanta, with Blank as the owner, and Atlanta United hit the field as a team for their first match in 2017. Blank was later approached by leaders in U.S. Soccer about supporting a training facility, with the possibility of putting it just outside his home city in the Peach State. "It doesn't take me long (to get on board). I love these young people that are here today and their dreams about being athletes at levels up that they'll continue to journey on," Blank said.
