How does FIFA determine what country a player is eligible for in the World Cup?
Source: {'href': 'https://uk.sports.yahoo.com', 'title': 'Yahoo! Sports UK'}    Published: 2026-06-03 10:07:37

A young midfielder from Alaska suiting up for Mexico? A defender from the Kansas City suburbs suiting up for Jordan? Those stories and more of crossing borders, families working abroad and, occasionally, savvy moves made by local soccer governing bodies all will be told at the FIFA World Cup, with all but eight teams participating in the tournament having at least one player born outside its borders representing their team. While there can be gray areas, FIFA's eligibility rules are relatively straight-forward. A player can represent the territory of their birth and also can represent the country of their parents' birth or the country of their grandparents' birth if they hold that nationality. Once a player sees time in an official FIFA competition, they are tied to that nationality. Article 9 of FIFA's regulations state: "A player may, only once, request to change the association for which heis eligible to play to the association of another country of which he holds the nationality." There are additional caveats that indicate whether or not the request to change will be approved. Players who have played more than three matches for another team will not be granted the switch. Once a player changes, they are locked into that team - hence the name "one-time change of association," sometimes called a "one-time switch." He has become an advocate for mental health awareness, helping children identify and overcome their struggles. “At any given time, there's probably five to 10 people around them feeling the exact same way,” Ream said.
