It's all an Optical Delusion: How to Spot a Conspiracy Theorist in the Wild
Source: {'href': 'https://eutoday.net', 'title': 'EU Today'}    Published: 2025-06-07 13:09:25

They walk among us, smiling benignly while secretly believing that the Moon landing was filmed in a warehouse just off the M25, that Greta Thunberg is a hologram generated by the Rothschild banking dynasty, and that Bill Gates is hiding inside your central heating system, collecting your biometric data every time you boil an egg. It's not that the modern conspiracy theorist is wrong about everything. It's the only currency accepted in their parallel universe, where logic is elitist, facts are fascist, and evidence is what you feel in your waters at 3am after half a bottle of gin and a YouTube binge.Take climate change. Now, to you and me, the notion that pumping billions of tonnes of carbon dioxide into the atmosphere for two centuries might just be warming the planet is not terribly controversial. The Earth, they say, has always gone through “natural cycles”—though strangely, these cycles now include wildfires in Sweden and beachfront properties in Sheffield.The climate change denier is a curious beast. He will confidently tell you, between puffs on his diesel-powered vape, (why do conspiracy theorists all seem to smoke vapes? ) that global warming is a hoax cooked up by Al Gore and lizard people to sell solar panels and vegan yoghurt. He is often the same man who believes George W. Bush personally detonated the Twin Towers using thermite, WiFi, and a remote control shaped like Dick Cheney's reproductive organ.Indeed, there seems to be a remarkable overlap in conspiracy circles. One moment you're reading a blog post titled “Chemtrails and the Cabbalistic Agenda of the Illuminati”, and the next it's a twelve-page screed explaining how Covid-19 was “a psy-op orchestrated by Klaus Schwab and the World Economic Forum to force us all to drink cricket smoothies and submit to compulsory yoga”.According to this crowd, the pandemic didn't really happen. The footage of overwhelmed Italian ICUs was staged—possibly by Stanley Kubrick's ghost. Ah, those were clearly designed to sterilise the population, implant nanobots, or possibly just make you slightly more left-wing.Of course, they'll say, “I'm just asking questions.” Which is conspiracy-speak for “I watched four hours of TikTok videos and now I have a PhD in virology, atmospheric science, and the globalist occult.” Asking questions is the sacred rite of the conspiracy theorist. But they never ask the one that matters: “What if I'm completely wrong and mildly unhinged?”Another sure giveaway is the use of capital letters where none are needed. “WAKE UP SHEEPLE!” they bellow in comment sections. OPEN YOUR EYES!” It's as though they've confused political awareness with being trapped in a malfunctioning Caps Lock key.And then, of course, there's the “New World Order”—the all-purpose bogeyman of the paranoid imagination. None other than the world's worst defence minister, Ursula von der Leyen.Yes, her.To suggest that Ursula von der Leyen is competent at her job is, to the conspiracy theorist, the ultimate shibboleth. Only they, the enlightened ones, understand that she's not a beleaguered bureaucrat wrestling with 27 bickering member states and a terminal shortage of talent. No, she is the High Priestess of the Euro-Soros-LGBT-Woke-Techno-Malthusian cult that secretly runs everything, including your local recycling rota.It's difficult to say how these ideas persist. You'd think, after 23 years of waiting for the “Deep State purge” promised by anonymous online accounts and guys called Dave with shaved heads and GoPros, they'd be a little chastened. Like a medieval plague cart, it trundles on regardless, ringing its bell and spreading a miasma of absolute nonsense.You try to reason with them, of course. You point out that the Earth is round, vaccines work, and that if the world really were run by a secret elite, we'd at least have functioning trains. Still clinging to your Newtonian physics and your parliamentary democracy.”Meanwhile, they continue their research—by which they mean watching an unshaven man in a caravan explain why 5G is turning us all into hummingbirds. Their definition of a “source” is “someone I agree with,” and their measure of truth is whether something “feels off”. Which is how we ended up with people earnestly claiming that Prince Philip faked his own death to return as a sentient cryptocurrency.Some of this might be funny if it weren't occasionally tragic. There are people who have lost friends, jobs, and even custody of their children because they genuinely believed they were resisting a global plot to enslave humanity via QR codes and oat milk. Conspiracy theories used to be fringe entertainment for stoners and shut-ins. Like bad manners and flatulence, conspiracy theories are probably with us to stay. And mock them we must.Because satire, unlike the internet, has not yet been monetised by lizards. And because the best disinfectant is not sunlight—it's ridicule.So the next time someone corners you at a barbecue to explain how a secret cabal of climate scientists faked 150 years of temperature data in order to tax your wood burner, take a deep breath, smile, and ask them gently: “If they're so powerful, why haven't they stopped you from talking?”And then offer them a drink. Every Time Death Appeared in Supernatural (& Why It Was So Important)
Source: {'href': 'https://www.cbr.com', 'title': 'Comic Book Resources'}    Published: 2025-06-07 04:10:00

With 15 years under its belt, The CW's Supernatural is packed full of memorable characters. However, few can equal the gravitas and impact of Death. The inherent power that oozes from actor Julian Richings has become legendary in Supernatural circles. Each appearance of Death carried significant weight and serious implications for the overarching storyline. In Supernatural, everything dies, and that doesn't happen without Death. Sporting a fancy car and heralded by his theme song, Death's stylish stride down the streets of Chicago is one of the most well-known scenes in the show's history. Death proves instrumental to Sam and Dean's fight against Lucifer in season 5. At their meeting in the pizzeria, Death reveals to Dean that he is much older than the universe itself and perhaps even older than God. This is a pivotal point in the season as Sam and Dean are gearing up for their showdown with the Devil. Dean first approaches with Death's scythe, which can reap any creature, in hopes of coercing Death to give up the ring, and Death makes the scythe disappear and reappear in his hand to a visibly shaken Dean. In the Supernatural mid-season finale of season 6, Dean seeks Death's help to restore Sam's soul from Lucifer's cage. Dean visits a colleague of his father's named Dr. Robert. He knows the only way to get a face-to-face meeting with Death at this point is if he does. Dr. Robert agrees to stop his heart and warns Dean that he has only about 3 minutes to conduct his business before the situation turns dire. Death seems amused yet entertains Dean's proposal to rescue Sam's soul from Hell, under one condition. Death's counteroffer is that Dean must take over his role for 24 hours. To teach Dean a lesson about the delicate nature of the circle of life and how upsetting it can have catastrophic results, he wanted Dean to see things from his perspective. The result is Dean realizing what a burden it is to bear Death's responsibility, and just how quickly things can go awry by deciding to save only one life that is destined to die. They're aware that a spell exists to bind Death to do their bidding because Lucifer could do so before the apocalypse. Crowley warns them they are playing with deadly forces, but ultimately gives them the scroll that lists the spells' ingredients. He is not happy at this turn of events. Castiel appears after the spell and threatens to kill them, but Dean says that is impossible since they have Death under control. This leads to Castiel breaking the spell, and a war of words ensues between the new "God" and Death. Death gets over his anger at the Winchesters, becoming increasingly annoyed by Castiel's arrogance. This is a key point in establishing the future villains of season 7, as Death warns Castiel that there are things older than souls in Purgatory, hinting at the presence of the Leviathans. His body is broken, and all the angels have fallen from Heaven because of Metatron's spell. Dean is searching for answers on how to save his brother, as Sam is unconscious in the hospital. Ezekiel offers to try and heal Sam before they are overrun by angels at their position. Dean enters Sam's psyche with help from the angel Ezekiel and pleads with him not to go with Death, ultimately tricking him into agreeing to allow Ezekiel to enter his body. Sam's choice not to go with Death and allow Ezekiel to heal him sets up the storyline for the entirety of Season 9 of Supernatural, as Sam and Dean become at odds after Sam discovers he was tricked into being possessed by an angel. Most of the season sees Dean struggle with the Mark of Cain and becoming prone to ever-increasing violent outbursts. He isolates himself from Sam and Castiel as they search for options to remove the Mark of Cain from him, deciding that using Rowena's Book of the Damned is their best option. The CW's Supernatural took fans on a ride for 15 seasons. With various complex plot devices and storylines, some aspects of the show remain a bit odd. Unfortunately for Dean, Death says that not even he can kill him because the Mark requires a host, as it houses a primordial being known as The Darkness. Sam finds Dean, and a brutal fight ensues. Dean killing Death is significant as now there must be a new Death, and since the Mark of Cain is removed without transferring hosts, Amara is unleashed, setting the primary antagonist for the following season. Billie was a reaper under the original Death's reign. She appears in the second episode of season 11, "Form and Void." She is singing the song "O' Death," which plays during the first on-screen appearance of Death in season 5. This hints at her eventual resurrection as Death in season 13. Billie, the reaper, is killed by Castiel in Season 12 to free the Winchesters from a blood oath they took from Billie. The episode begins as a typical monster-of-the-week Supernatural special. Sam and Dean investigate a haunted sanatorium where two boys were attacked and discover the ghost of an evil doctor still haunts the building, keeping the ghosts of his former patients hostage. To find the bodies of the ghosts and set their spirits free, Dean injects himself with medication to stop his heart. He then encounters Billie in her new job as Death. She informs him that now is not his time to die and that he and Sam have a lot of work to do. This establishes Billie as a major player, as she now has access to all of death's powers and abilities. Rowena is on a rampage as she's traveling through the northwest and murdering the upper management of major pharmaceutical companies. Jessica, a reaper assigned to spy on Sam and Dean by Death, tells them she is upsetting the natural balance of the universe by killing people before their time is due, and this can have cosmic consequences. Rowena reveals to Sam and Dean that she is trying to get Death's attention by killing her reapers. Billie is not the Death of old, who almost found it amusing for people to return from the dead. Billie appears before Rowena, and the witch attacks her with all of her magic, but it does nothing to Death.
