Getting a little extra muscle in the conflict likely required having a bit of spare cash on hand, and archaeologists recently uncovered some of that loot in the form of a hoard of gold coins, which they found buried in a small pot in western Turkey. According to researchers led by the University of Michigan, the gold coins—which were originally discovered in 2023, but only recently okayed for publication by the Turkish Ministry of Culture and Tourism—depict a kneeling archer. The fact that this literal pot of gold was even found shows that an unlikely scenario befell the owner. “The discovery of such a valuable find in a controlled archaeological excavation is very rare,” said Christopher Ratte, professor of ancient Mediterranean art and archaeology and director of the Notion Archaeological Survey, the project that discovered the coins, in a statement.
