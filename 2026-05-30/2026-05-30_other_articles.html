White House Website Equates Immigrants with Aliens and Invites Them to be Reported
Source: {'href': 'https://colombiaone.com', 'title': 'ColombiaOne.com'}    Published: 2026-05-30 02:23:00

The world entered a state of expectation over what else might be uncovered on a subject of absolute interest to humanity. In that context, the White House has just made a publication in which it compares migrants to aliens. That site is different from another one that the White House has just opened for a similar purpose. That narrative made it possible to create a short video in which a UFO abducts a human in Mexican territory, carries them over the air across the border barrier wall, and then drops them onto U.S. soil. “They Walk Among Us” is the title of the suggestive video, over which a text states that “for 60 years, the U.S. government has kept a closely guarded secret. Extraterrestrials have been walking among us, living in our neighborhoods and interacting with us in our daily lives. They have shopped in the same stores, attended the same classes as our children, and led seemingly normal human lives. With one exception: they do not belong here.” According to the text, “millions” of these ‘aliens' “arrived under the cover of darkness and integrated directly into our society. Countless presidents, congressmen, and senior officials knew exactly what was happening. Instead of protecting American citizens, they chose to cover it up and even accelerate the invasion,” it adds, in line with the widely held idea among large sectors around the world that the United States has for years concealed the existence of extraterrestrials and evidence related to them. President Trump was the first to expose the real danger that aliens pose to every American family, every community, and the future of our nation. The truth is no longer out there. All of this leads to the bottom of the page, where a counter appears showing the number of “encounters” carried out, referring to the number of ICE arrests, a figure that currently amounts to 3.1 million people. The portal also allows users to report “suspicious aliens” through a hyperlink that connects to the ICE website: “If you have witnessed an alien abduction, do not be alarmed. The alien is in good hands, we will return them safely to their place of origin.” As expected, the website has sparked all kinds of criticism from those who consider it openly violative of human rights and deeply discriminatory. The term “aliens” refers to “foreigners,” but not only extraterrestrials. It is a concept used in immigration law to define foreign nationals. The Cambridge Dictionary defines it as “coming from a country, ethnicity, or group different from another.” Thus, it is a play on words at the center of the website's design, which moves citizens, from their subconscious, to denounce migrants for deportation.
