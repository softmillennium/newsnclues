The obsession of the US Vice President, JD Vance, with extraterrestrial life: “I believe they are demons”
Source: {'href': 'https://www.democrata.es', 'title': 'democrata.es'}    Published: 2026-03-30 16:54:00

The US vice president, JD Vance, affirms that extraterrestrial phenomena could be “demons” The Vice President of the United States, JD Vance, has surprised with his statements on an American podcast by mentioning that aliens could be “demons”. The revelation took place on the conservative podcast Benny Show, where Vance claimed to be “obsessed” with unidentified flying objects, currently called UAP (unidentified anomalous phenomena, for its acronym in English). Vance, who identifies as a deeply religious person, explained that his interest is not limited to mere scientific curiosity, but is part of a broader debate about good and evil and the religious interpretation of inexplicable phenomena. “I don't believe they are extraterrestrials, I believe they are demons, but that is a longer debate,” he went on to state. “All the great religions of the world, including Christianity, in which I believe, have understood that strange and difficult-to-explain things exist. And when I hear about some extranatural phenomenon, I resort to the Christian understanding that there is much good, but there is also evil,” he added. Vance's interest comes at a time when previous political figures, such as Barack Obama and Donald Trump, have also generated headlines about UAPs. Obama initially stated that aliens were “real,” although he later qualified his statements by pointing out that there was no evidence during his presidency. Trump, for his part, announced on Truth Social the opening of government files about extraterrestrial life and inexplicable phenomena.


A CW Show's Best Episode Is Responsible For Star Wars' Worst Movie
Source: {'href': 'https://www.giantfreakinrobot.com', 'title': 'Giant Freakin Robot'}    Published: 2026-03-30 16:31:54

Supernatural felt like it was on the air forever, 15 seasons and 327 episodes is an incredible run, but it's even more impressive for a genre show. That's why it's hard to remember, but way back in the second episode, “Wendigo,” Alden Ehrenreich made his acting debut as the young Ben Collins, and while he'd go on to star in Weapons, Ironheart, and Beautiful Creatures, his most infamous role was no fault of his own. Ehrenreich starred as young Han Solo in Solo, the most unneeded prequel of all time, and a film that destroyed the entire Star Wars side-story project Disney had been pursuing, which is a little ironic, as his very on-screen role was so good, it helped turn Supernatural into the juggernaut it would become. Alden Ehrenreich's Ben Collins is wandering in the wilderness with his older sister, Hayley, as they try to find their other brother, Tommy, who went missing. Sam (Jared Padalecki) and Dean (Jensen Ackles) find the siblings, and thanks to their dad's journal, deduce it was a Wendigo that took Tommy. The Wendigo they find isn't a furry, bestial monster, instead it's closer to the actual tradition, a strange-looking, gaunt, humanoid that has more in common with Slender Man than Big Foot, but it's also one of the best-looking monsters of Season 1. The episode plays out as you'd expect, with Sam and Dean losing horribly to the beast before they outsmart it and snatch victory from its ravenous jaws of defeat. Supernatural eventually got stuck in a rut with subsequent episodes playing out in the same fashion, but given its later seasons' emphasis on the mythology arc, it's refreshing to go back and see such a simple Monster of the Week story that lets Sam and Dean be, well, Sam and Dean, going up against a beast from classic folklore. Following the success of “Wendigo,” Supernatural became a hit, and Alden Ehrenreich hasn't had to stop working yet. Supernatural fans should at least check out Beautiful Creatures, based on the young adult novel of the same name, where he plays a teen who falls in love with a young witch. It's not Ehrenreich's fault that Solo was a failure. Asking him to play one of the most iconic roles in movie history that made Harrison Ford a movie star was an impossible task. The fact that he does as well as he does is a testament to his acting ability and his own natural charm. Supernatural had to overcome its own periods of studio interference, and its spin-off, The Winchesters, also had a rough start to production.
