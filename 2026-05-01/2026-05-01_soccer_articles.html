Source: {'href': 'https://www.abc15.com', 'title': 'ABC15 Arizona'}    Published: 2026-05-01 00:35:00

As the 2026 FIFA World Cup approaches, beginning June 11th in cities across North America, Mesa will become an international enclave for Turkish fans and athletes alike. The news has invigorated Mesa's Turkish community, including business owners Mustafa Uygur and his son, Ali, who operate the Microfix24 repair shop near Arizona State University. Originally from Istanbul, the Uygur family has called Tempe home for nearly a decade. I'm almost six or seven thousand miles away, and they're coming here. The practice field is going to be just a 19-minute drive from my house. It's a dream come true,” Mustafa said, unable to contain his enthusiasm. “Hopefully, we can go visit them at their practices here in Mesa. Maybe if I can convince him,” Ali joked, hinting at a family outing. Two of Turkey's three group stage contests will be held in California, including a highly anticipated June 25 match against the U.S. national team. The economic impact isn't lost on local entrepreneurs like Mustafa “Moos” Erdem, owner of Real Food Kitchen, a Mesa restaurant popular among Turkish soccer fans. “Hopefully, we're going to have a good time, and they will enjoy Arizona.” For them, and for soccer enthusiasts throughout the Valley, the chance to see their national team up close represents an unprecedented opportunity. However, FIFA has yet to release a detailed local training schedule and has not responded to ABC15's request for comment.
