Since coming up with Ryan Coogler and Chloé Zhao, the Texan filmmaker has directed what might be his most ambitious project yet. That's how Texas-raised and Dallas-based filmmaker David Lowery began his pitch to A24 for Mother Mary, which stars Anne Hathaway as the titular troubled pop diva and Emmy winner Michaela Coel as Sam Anselm, her estranged friend and former creative partner. A revered fashion designer, Sam is tasked with making the dress Mother Mary will wear at her comeback performance—but you're never quite sure whether she's going to finish the outfit or take her shiny, oversized shears and stab Hathaway in the heart. Despite that attempt, this haunting, fantastical movie is impossible to capture in a few words. It has a pop soundtrack with original songs by Jack Antonoff, Charli xcx, and FKA twigs (who also plays a psychic medium); gorgeous costumes by Oscar nominee Bina Daigeler; and concert scenes in which Hathaway's onstage theatrics rival anything Beyoncé or Lady Gaga has pulled off. Lowery found inspiration for it in Taylor Swift, Lorde, and Catholic iconography. I first met Lowery in Birmingham, Alabama, at the 2007 Sidewalk Film Festival. Since then, he's gone from an unassuming and unknown writer-director to one of the most imaginative, versatile filmmakers working today. His breakout feature, Ain't Them Bodies Saints, won two awards at Sundance in 2013; he directed a blockbuster three years later with Pete's Dragon and then returned to small-budget projects with 2017's A Ghost Story, his first film with A24. I spoke to Lowery about working with Hathaway and Coel, getting the cinema bug as a kid in Irving, and the bonds he formed with Oscar winners Chloé Zhao and Ryan Coogler at the Sundance Screenwriters Lab, several years before they all became filmmakers with enough power and clout to make movies that defy all mainstream-marketing logic. I made models, little sets, all the ephemera required to make a movie. I was obsessed with crafting, and that hasn't changed.
