In a ruling published on December 17, the ICO said it could have taken the council to the High Court for contempt of court but chose not to because of how "lengthy" it would have been, which would have caused further delays to the investigation. A Lambeth Council spokesperson said it will respond to the ICO report "in due course", adding that it was committed to openness and transparency in all of its work and ensuring it is held to the highest possible standards. The resident had submitted a Freedom of Information (FoI) request to the council on November 28, 2024 which asked for pre-application information relating to the potential redevelopment of Bellevue Garden Mansions in Stockwell. The plans would have seen the loss of a communal garden, a local nursery and a green space which contains basketball courts which are heavily used by the Stockwell community. Hyde's plans had been met with strong opposition from residents living at Bellevue Garden Mansions and the wider Stockwell community (Image: Peter Arkell ) The FoI sought all pre-application advice given by Lambeth Council to Hyde Housing regarding the development, as well as any emails, letters or meeting minutes between council officers and third-party representatives. The resident requested for several other details, including records of any site visits by council officers and evidence of any initial plans, proposals or concept designs. The council responded on January 3, 2025, confirming that it held relevant information but ultimately rejected the resident's request, citing environmental and commercial exceptions. Bellevue Garden Mansions is located on Clapham Road, Lambeth (Image: Google Street View ) The ICO contacted the council in July of last year and asked for a copy of the information it was withholding as well as legal arguments which supported its reasons for withholding information on commercial and environmental grounds. The council was legally required to respond to the Information Notice within 30 days, but did not respond until 48 days later. A spokesperson for residents who are involved with the FOI request told the Local Democracy Reporting Service (LDRS): "Lambeth Council seems to be avoiding this issue. They wouldn't even let us speak for three minutes at a recent council meeting. "The original stock transfer document between Lambeth and Hyde Housing guaranteed that Hyde would maintain and repair Bellevue Garden Mansions until 2029, not demolish them. We eagerly await Lambeth Council's response to the ICO's ruling on this matter." The ICO has ordered Lambeth Council to disclose the information it withheld and must take action within 30 days of the ICO issuing its decision otherwise it could face High Court action. A spokesperson for Lambeth Council told the LDRS: "We receive over 3,000 FoI requests every year, and we work hard to respond to all of these fully – in compliance with regulations and within statutory timescales.
