Supernatural Quietly Used a Genius Trick to Turn TV's Worst Trope Into Franchise Gold
Source: {'href': 'https://www.cbr.com', 'title': 'Comic Book Resources'}    Published: 2025-11-11 09:45:00

There are many reasons why The CW's Supernatural lasted for a historic 15 seasons. However, the show's biggest storytelling trick was taking a somewhat dated Monster-of-the-Week approach as a foundation and infusing it with key moments of character development to connect the ever-expanding mythology. Supernatural turned this perceived weakness into its strength, nearly eliminating episodes that felt like filler. Each Monster-of-the-Week entry established the lore that the hunting universe of Sam and Dean Winchester was built upon. Especially early in the series, each hunt felt like its own campfire tale, episodes that viewers could follow and understand if it were their first time catching the show.
