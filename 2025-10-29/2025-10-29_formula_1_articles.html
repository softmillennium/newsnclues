Louis Tomlinson, Kane Brown and more to headline Las Vegas Grand Prix grid entertainment
Source: {'href': 'https://www.formula1.com', 'title': 'Formula 1'}    Published: 2025-10-29 16:16:32

Mexico City Lap 1 chaos with Alex Jacques and Jolyon Palmer | The Cooldown Room A number of headline acts have been announced for pre and post-race entertainment at the Las Vegas Grand Prix. VAVO, DJ Pee .Wee, Steve Aoki, Kaskade and more will also join the line-up for a host of performances pre and post-race when F1 hits the streets of Las Vegas on Saturday, November 22. Tomlinson, who rose to fame with One Direction, recently announced his third studio solo album, HOW DID I GET HERE?, set for release on January 23, 2026. His world tour starts in spring 2026, with North American arena dates commencing June 3. VAVO leads the next evolution of electronic music with globally viral remixes of country music hits and high-profile original collaborations such as Bullet and World on Fire (VAVO Remix). Known for his surprise appearances at major events like Coachella and the Met Gala, DJ Pee .Wee is set to deliver an unforgettable, groove-heavy experience blending funk, R&B throwbacks, and diverse dance tracks. DJ and producer Kaskade will close out race night with a post-race performance following the podium celebration. Select ticket holders will have live views of the grid performances, with exclusive access and premium vantage points included in designated hospitality areas. “Las Vegas has always been a global stage for entertainment, and this year's grid line-up reflects that energy,” said Emily Prazer, President and CEO of Las Vegas Grand Prix, Inc. “From Kane Brown's chart-topping country numbers and Louis Tomlinson's pop hits to Steve Aoki's electrifying sets and Kaskade's legendary beats, fans will witness a soundtrack as exhilarating as the race itself.” Meanwhile, at the Heineken Stage, fans can look forward to dynamic sets from Shaggy and Dillon Francis, supported by performances from Jazzy, BANDABBA, Awakening, Jabbawockeez, Cirque du Soleil's Mad Apple, and additional special guests.
