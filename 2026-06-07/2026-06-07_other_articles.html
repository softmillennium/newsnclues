United States Declassifies Documents About UFOs, but Nothing Conclusive Yet
Source: {'href': 'https://colombiaone.com', 'title': 'ColombiaOne.com'}    Published: 2026-06-07 01:00:00

The announcement has been more sensationalist and far less revealing. The United States Department of Defense began this Friday to comply with President Donald Trump's order to disclose documents held by the US government containing information on Unidentified Aerial Phenomena (UAP). Thus, this Friday dozens of PDF documents, videos and images labeled with the attractive description “never seen before” were released to the public, a label that will always attract curiosity. All of this is enriching the website war.gov/ufo, which will be updated each week with new material. It also includes photographs taken from the Moon during that last mission, never seen before, as well as transcripts of communications between the spacecraft's pilots. The publication of these documents had been announced by President Trump last February on his social network Truth. There he expressed his desire to release materials such as those published this Friday. “In view of the great interest shown, I will direct the Secretary of War [Pete Hegseth] and other relevant departments and agencies to begin the process of identifying and releasing government files related to extraterrestrial life, unidentified aerial phenomena and unidentified flying objects, as well as any other information related to these highly complex but extremely interesting and important matters,” he wrote at the time. “With these new documents and videos, people can decide for themselves: ‘WHAT THE HELL IS GOING ON?'. Have fun and enjoy!” he wrote, as if promoting a movie or television program. The process of publishing these documents was accelerated after an exchange of statements with Barack Obama, who had said in a podcast that these phenomena “are real,” although he denied having evidence of secret installations. For that reason, he decided to move ahead by publishing the entire catalog in order to demonstrate the supposed “total transparency” of his administration. The publication of the documents is also seen by Trump's critics as a smokescreen to distract public attention at a time when the president is recording very low approval ratings, breaking historic lows, due, among other things, to the already prolonged war against Iran, which he promised to end within weeks but which has continued with dangerous consequences not only for the United States (including rising gasoline prices), but also for the global economy. On the contrary, skeptics will continue waiting for that image, that unmistakably clear video, which proves the existence of life in outer space. The US Air Force dispatched an F-16 on Feb. 12, 2023 to confront the apparent "invading" orb over Lake Huron, blowing it to smithereens with what was likely an AIM-9 Sidewinder missile, according to video released last month by the Department of War as part of its second batch of UFO files. "The F16 shot at a balloon over Lake Huron. After the [Chinese spy] balloon embarrassment, DOD was shooting at every [Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena] they detected," Tim Phillips, a former interim director of AARO, told The Post. The object belonged to a Boy Scout troop, according to Sean Kirkpatrick, a former head of the federal All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office, or AARO. "[The balloon] had circumnavigated the globe eight times before we shot it down with a half-million-dollar missile," Kirkpatrick said at a conference on April 27. "You can imagine the response on the Hill when I briefed that," he said at that conference. The orb was part of an ongoing, unspecified research project, Kirkpatrick added. The Biden administration wasted more ammunition shooting down another hobbyist balloon on Feb 11, 2023 over Alaska. Many of the supposed UFO videos produced by the military, some of which are being released by the Trump administration, depict standard manmade objects that were misidentified by Reaper drones operators. And you've got a bored operator looking around. "We're actually seeing, good reporting coming out of the fleet, coming out of the operation wings and squadrons, where they're willing to report this stuff. And this is part of that outreach where we get our scientists to talk to the Reaper operators and actually go side by side and try to do some education," he said.
