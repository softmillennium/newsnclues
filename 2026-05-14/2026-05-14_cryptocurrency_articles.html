The BOK's decision, reported by iNews24, reflects a cautious yet forward-looking strategy. This ordering suggests the BOK aims to ensure the underlying payment rails are secure, efficient, and scalable before introducing assets that could introduce new forms of financial risk. For market participants, the BOK's stance provides a clearer regulatory horizon. Financial institutions exploring tokenization may need to align their strategies with the central bank's priorities, focusing first on digital payment infrastructure rather than asset tokenization. The warning about liquidity risks also serves as a cautionary note for investors and platforms developing tokenized asset products, highlighting the need for robust risk management frameworks that account for the differences between digital and traditional settlement cycles.
