The stench of the washer may be harming you more than you think - see how to solve
Source: gizmodo    Published: 2025-03-23 17:19:49

If your washer has released an unpleasant smell right after each washing cycle, know that this discomfort has explanation - and solution. Dirt, accumulated humidity and lack of proper ventilation are the main responsible for bad odor, which may even transfer to clothes. Fortunately, with simple cleaning and conscious use, it is possible to end this problem and improve machine performance. According to the organization of consumers and users (occupation) of Spain, the main cause of the bad smell is related to the accumulation of dirt and moisture in internal parts of the machine, such as the filter and the sealing rubber. These waste favor the proliferation of bacteria and fungi, especially when the washer is kept closed after use, preventing air circulation. Over time, this damp and muffled environment favors the appearance of mold and bad smell, which also eventually impregnate clothes. To solve the problem, the first step is to do a deep cleaning. Occupa recommends sanitizing the filter and rubber of the door, which accumulate detergent residues, leaks and various dirt. The filter should be taken carefully and cleaned with running water, removing any obstruction. Rubber can be cleaned with a damp cloth and white vinegar or baking soda, which help eliminate mold and neutralize odors. After manual cleaning, make an empty wash cycle, with water at high temperature (between 70 ° C and 90 ° C), adding sodium baking soda in the soap compartment. This process helps disinfect the interior of the drum and remove waste that is not visible to the naked eye. If the bad smell has already contaminated the clothes, it is important to wash them again at high temperature-since the fabric allows it-using a cup of baking soda in place of the traditional soap. Bicarbonate acts as a natural cleaning agent, eliminating odors and waste without assaulting the fabric fibers. Another effective option is to add white vinegar during rinse. Its disinfectant properties help remove impurities and neutralize persistent odors. Just avoid exaggeration as overuse can damage long-term clothing. After washing, hang the clothes in the sun in a well-ventilated place. Sunlight helps eliminate moisture and prevent the bad smell from returning. Prevention is the key to keeping the washer always clean and efficient. One of the most common errors is excessive use of soap. Following the manufacturer's instructions on dosage prevents accumulation of waste and internal obstructions. Leaving the washer and soap compartment open after each use is also essential. This practice allows the machine to vent and dry completely, avoiding the emergence of mold. It is recommended to do hot water washing cycles periodically, especially if you usually use low temperature programs that do not eliminate residues effectively. Occupa recommends cleaning the filter at least once a month and regularly check the rubber and soap compartment.

"All whales showed the lowest levels of vocalization when ocean conditions were in the worst condition." These songs fulfill various vital functions: they serve for communication, space orientation, social interaction, and even to locate food. Their absence represents more than a behavioral change - it is a sign of environmental alert. Direct impacts of seas warming The analysis showed that as ocean temperature increases and prey availability decreases, whales are forced to migrate to new areas in search of food. This requires more energy, increases stress, and directly affects its reproduction and survival capacity. Source: wired    Published: 2025-03-23 13:00:00

Companies employ Bluetooth in many proprietary features, like Apple’s AirDrop and Google’s Quick Share. The U1 chip in Apple devices enables ultra-wideband (UWB) technology to determine the direction of your phone and help you share files with nearby devices, AirDrop uses Bluetooth for the initial discovery process and then switches to Wi-Fi to transmit files. Google merged Nearby Share with Samsung’s Quick Share in 2024, and much like AirDrop, it uses Bluetooth to establish a connection and then Wi-Fi to transfer files. These are just two examples. Google also has a standard called Fast Pair that quickly connects a wireless device with a smartphone, and it uses Bluetooth too. Bluetooth is named after King Harald “Bluetooth” Gormsson—the logo is a combination of his runes for his initials—who had a literal blue tooth (it was dead). He united Denmark and Norway in 958. This short-range radio technology grew from a 1996 meeting between Intel, Ericsson, and Nokia. The standard is managed by a not-for-profit Bluetooth Special Interest Group, or Bluetooth SIG, and it's headquartered in the US. While there are thousands of members, the most influential companies help shape the direction of the standard and include companies like Ericsson, Intel, Nokia, Apple, Microsoft, Lenovo, and Google. Every version brought myriad incremental improvements to make Bluetooth faster, more efficient, more secure, and less buggy, but we’re just going to run through the headline features here. Bluetooth 1.2 in 2003 figured out how to avoid Wi-Fi interference and enabled quicker pairing, helping to popularize the standard. Bluetooth 2.0 in 2004 brought EDR (Enhanced Data Rate), tripling the data rate from 1 to 3 Mbps. Bluetooth 2.1 in 2007 brought SSP (Secure Simple Pairing), enhancing security and reducing power draw. Bluetooth 3.0 + HS (High Speed) in 2009 bumped rates up by initially connecting via Bluetooth and then switching to Wi-Fi. Bluetooth 4.0 LE (Low Energy) landed in 2010 and, as the name suggests, reduced power requirements even further. Bluetooth 4.1 in 2013 allowed Bluetooth devices to communicate with each other. Bluetooth 4.2 boosted data rates and IoT connectivity in 2014. Bluetooth 5 in 2016 increased the potential range massively, going from around 50 meters to well over 200 meters. But range is dependent on the power class, and there are three classes. Class 1 devices have the greatest range and use the most power, Class 2 are limited to around 10 meters, and Class 3 is only 1 meter. Bluetooth 5.1 in 2019 brought faster pairing and better tracking accuracy. Bluetooth 5.2 in 2020 allowed for multiple simultaneous data streams and better control over the power and signal required by connected devices. Bluetooth 5.3 in 2021 brought improvements in audio quality, increased power efficiency, and wider compatibility. Bluetooth 5.4 in 2023 improved security and reduced latency, but it also included functionality aimed at retailers, allowing them to monitor and change electronic shelf labels (ESL). Devices that support ESL are expected to ship in 2027. Bluetooth 6.0 is the latest version, announced in 2024. It includes a precise tracking capability called Channel Sounding that enables two devices to accurately measure the distance between them, alongside various efficiency improvements. One of the main reasons Bluetooth is so popular is because it's easy to use. You can generally turn Bluetooth on via the settings of your phone or computer and find a menu of available devices to connect with. This feature would allow Apple Watch users to quickly receive information about items or places their surroundings. For example, they could point their Apple Watch at a restaurant to receive its hours or ratings as they pass by on the sidewalk. Visual Intelligence debuted on all iPhone 16 models last year, and it is available on iPhone 15 Pro models starting with iOS 18.4. Apple is also planning to add tiny cameras to future AirPods models, he said. The cameras will bring more AI capabilities to both the Apple Watch and AirPods, allowing Apple to better compete with other AI-powered wearables on the market.
