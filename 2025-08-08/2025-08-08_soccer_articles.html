Gonzalo Garcia has signed new Real Madrid contract after breakthrough Club World Cup
Source: {'href': 'https://www.nytimes.com', 'title': 'The New York Times'}    Published: 2025-08-08 19:36:28

Gonzalo Garcia has signed a new Real Madrid contract through to 2030. The Athletic reported in the DealSheet on Tuesday Garcia's agent was working on an extension and an improvement to his deal after his impressive performances at the Club World Cup this summer. The plan this summer had initially been for the Spain youth international to depart in search of regular playing time, and he had doubts over his future at Madrid due to Endrick, 19, competing for the same position. But after enjoying an impressive Club World Cup, he is expected to remain with the first team, with Xabi Alonso an important factor in his decision to sign a new deal. Not even Gonzalo Garcia could have predicted his past two months. Before the Club World Cup, the 21-year-old's standout moment in a Real Madrid shirt had been a late header against Leganes to send his side through to the Copa del Rey semi-finals in February. But his achievements in the United States made everyone sit up and take notice. His goal against Juventus was the pick of the bunch, a guided header from a Trent Alexander-Arnold cross that drew comparisons with Madrid great Raul. Those were no surprise given the legendary former striker was Gonzalo's coach last season with Real Madrid Castilla, when he scored a record 25 goals in a campaign for the reserve side. Gonzalo revealed after the Juventus game that Raul still texts him after every match and called it “a great compliment to be compared to him” while “respecting the distances” between them. Nobody is suggesting Gonzalo can reach those levels just yet. But with Endrick's form erratic and Jude Bellingham absent after his shoulder surgery, the kid from the academy could be just what Xabi Alonso needs to break down stubborn opponents. Netflix Takes Over FIFA Women's World Cup Broadcasts in United States and Canada
Source: {'href': 'https://www.si.com', 'title': 'Sports Illustrated'}    Published: 2025-08-08 19:16:15

The FIFA Women's World Cup is coming off traditional television channels in the United States and Canada and joining Netflix. It marks the first time that Netflix has taken on the rights to live soccer, and it joins the likes of DAZN, Apple TV, Amazon Prime, Fubo and other streaming services to take on live matches in a step towards a new era of sports watching. “We are thrilled that Netflix will be FIFA's exclusive partner in Canada for the Women's World Cup in 2027 and 2031,” said Gabe Spitzer, Netflix's vice-president of sports. “With under two years to go until the first match in Brazil, we are hard at work on planning and aim to give fans unparalleled access to the most prestigious competition in women's football.” At the same time, the DAZN Club World Cup package included studio and original programming, unlike many streaming services that take on sporting properties. Netflix has previously broadcast live sports, notably with the Mike Tyson vs. Jake Paul boxing match in 2024. However, that fight featured the service cutting out for several users, among other technical problems that will have to be sorted out for the World Cups and potential other properties. In addition to Paul vs. Tyson, Netflix has broadcast live Christmas Day NFL games, and the Katie Taylor vs. Amanda Serrano fight card, with Netflix saying the latter drew 74 million live viewers worldwide. “We will embrace the opportunity to work with Netflix to continue to grow the popularity of the women's game in Canada — a nation with a rich FIFA Women's World Cup history,” FIFA chief business officer Romy Gai said in a statement. Brazil is set to host the 2027 Women's World Cup, while the 2031 tournament is in its bidding process, with U.S. Soccer confirming its hosting interest in conjunction with other Concacaf nations. U.S. Travel Association raises visa concerns ahead of FIFA World Cup, Olympics
Source: {'href': 'https://www.sportsbusinessjournal.com', 'title': 'Sports Business Journal'}    Published: 2025-08-08 15:27:49

The U.S. Travel Association is “raising concerns about a new cost” with which foreign travelers coming to the U.S. for the 2026 FIFA World Cup and the 2028 L.A. Games will have to contend, according to Daniel Barnes of POLITICO. Under a new law, the Department of Homeland Security “must start charging visa applicants a $250 fee at the time their application is approved.” The fee will be “revised annually to keep up with inflation,” meaning it “could be higher by the time” of LA28. The Congressional Budget Office “estimated the fee would raise around” $28.9B over the next decade. The travel industry group on Friday announced the “launch of the America's Sports and Travel Mega Event Coalition,” a new effort to “support the president's focus on the mega sporting events” and urge administration officials to “delay implementation of the visa fee or lean on Congress to remove it altogether.” Monument Advocacy, which has worked with the U.S. Travel Association since 2006, will lead the lobbying efforts. The coalition, which includes Marriott International, InterContinental Hotels Group (IHG), American Airlines and others, also “plans to push for increased U.S. consular staffing and expanded hours of operation to make the visa process more efficient” (POLITICO, 8/7).
